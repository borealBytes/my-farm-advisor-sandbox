## The GED Test Overview

The GED test consists of four subjects:
1. **Mathematical Reasoning** (115 minutes, 46 questions)
   - Basic math, geometry, algebra, graphs
   - Calculator allowed (TI-30XS provided onscreen)
   - Two parts: Part 1 (no calculator, 5 questions, 15 min), Part 2 (calculator, 41 questions, 100 min)

2. **Reasoning Through Language Arts** (150 minutes, 3 sections)
   - Reading comprehension, grammar, writing
   - One written essay (extended response)
   - Combination of multiple choice, drag-and-drop, hotspot

3. **Science** (90 minutes, 40 questions)
   - Life science, physical science, earth/space science
   - Reading charts, graphs, experiments
   - No memorization required - all information provided

4. **Social Studies** (70 minutes, 35 questions)
   - Civics, government, US history, economics, geography
   - Similar to science - information provided in passages
   - One extended response essay

**Passing Score:** 145+ per subject (max 200)
**Total Cost:** Approximately $120-160 (varies by state)
**Retakes:** Discounted retakes available

## 8-Week Study Schedule

### Week 1: Foundation Building
**Theme:** Diagnostic review and fundamentals

**Mathematical Reasoning (3 hours):**
- Monday (1 hour): Number operations review
  - Whole numbers, fractions, decimals, percents
  - Order of operations (PEMDAS)
  - Practice: Khan Academy - Arithmetic
- Wednesday (1 hour): Introduction to algebra
  - Variables, expressions, equations
  - Solving one-step equations
  - Practice: GED Academy or Essential Education
- Friday (1 hour): Word problem strategies
  - Translating words to math
  - Estimation techniques
  - Practice: 10 word problems from GED.com

**Reasoning Through Language Arts (3 hours):**
- Tuesday (1 hour): Reading comprehension strategies
  - Main idea identification
  - Supporting details
  - Inference techniques
  - Practice: Read news articles, summarize
- Thursday (1 hour): Grammar fundamentals
  - Parts of speech
  - Sentence structure
  - Common errors (run-ons, fragments)
  - Practice: Purdue OWL exercises
- Saturday (1 hour): Extended response introduction
  - Essay structure (introduction, body, conclusion)
  - Reading and analyzing source texts
  - Practice: Outline one essay

**Science & Social Studies (2 hours combined):**
- Sunday (2 hours): Understanding the format
  - How to read charts/graphs
  - Experiment analysis
  - Timeline and map reading
  - Practice: 5 questions from each subject

**Weekly Goals:**
- [ ] Complete 50 math practice problems
- [ ] Read and summarize 3 articles
- [ ] Write one practice essay outline
- [ ] Review 20 science/social studies questions

### Week 2: Math Deep Dive - Part 1
**Theme:** Algebra and quantitative problem solving

**Mathematical Reasoning (5 hours):**
- Monday (1.5 hours): Linear equations
  - Solving multi-step equations
  - Linear inequalities
  - Word problems with equations
  - Example: "If 3x + 7 = 22, what is x?"
  - Example: "Sarah has $50 and saves $10/week. How many weeks to reach $150?"
  
- Wednesday (1.5 hours): Functions and graphing
  - Linear functions (y = mx + b)
  - Reading graphs and tables
  - Slope and intercepts
  - Practice: Graph 5 linear functions
  
- Friday (2 hours): Quadratic equations
  - Solving by factoring
  - Quadratic formula
  - Word problems
  - Calculator skills (TI-30XS)
  - Practice: 10 quadratic problems

**Reasoning Through Language Arts (2 hours):**
- Tuesday (1 hour): Close reading practice
  - Annotating texts
  - Identifying tone and purpose
  - Author's point of view
  
- Thursday (1 hour): Grammar review continued
  - Pronouns and antecedents
  - Subject-verb agreement
  - Parallel structure

**Science & Social Studies (2 hours):**
- Review practice questions
- Focus on weakest areas from Week 1

**Resources:**
- Khan Academy: Algebra Basics
- GED.com: Free practice questions
- YouTube: "GED Math Crash Course" (recommended channels: Light & Salt Learning, The Magic Carpenter)

## Success Tips for Adult Learners

1. **Consistency over intensity** - Study 1 hour daily rather than 8 hours weekly
2. **Find a study buddy** - Accountability helps
3. **Use multiple resources** - Don't rely on one book
4. **Practice under test conditions** - Timed, quiet environment
5. **Celebrate small wins** - Each practice test improvement
6. **Connect to goals** - Remember why you're doing this
7. **Don't compare** - Everyone's journey is different
8. **Ask for help** - Teachers, tutors, online forums
9. **Take care of yourself** - Sleep, nutrition, exercise
10. **Believe in yourself** - You can do this!
