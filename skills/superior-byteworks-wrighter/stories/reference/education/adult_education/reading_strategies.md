# Reading Strategies

## The Reading Process

### Three Stages

**Before Reading (Preview):**
- Activate prior knowledge
- Set purpose
- Preview structure
- Make predictions

**During Reading (Active Reading):**
- Engage with text
- Monitor understanding
- Take notes
- Ask questions

**After Reading (Review):**
- Summarize
- Reflect
- Connect
- Apply

## Previewing (5-10 Minutes)

### Why Preview?
- Activates background knowledge
- Sets purpose for reading
- Creates mental framework
- Improves focus

### How to Preview

**1. Look at Title:**
- What do you already know about this topic?
- What questions do you have?
- {{TITLE_PREDICTION}}

**2. Read Headings:**
- What is the structure?
- What will you learn?
- {{HEADING_OVERVIEW}}

**3. Check Visuals:**
- Look at charts, graphs, images
- Read captions
- What do they tell you?
- {{VISUAL_SUMMARY}}

**4. Read First and Last Paragraphs:**
- Introduction sets up topic
- Conclusion summarizes main points
- {{INTRO_SUMMARY}}

**5. Read Summary/Abstract:**
- If available, read first
- Gives overview of entire text

## Active Reading Strategies

### 1. SQ3R Method

**Survey:** Preview the text (as above)

**Question:**
- Turn headings into questions
- Write questions in margins
- Example: Heading "The Water Cycle" → "What is the water cycle?"

**Read:**
- Read to answer your questions
- Look for main ideas
- Take notes

**Recite:**
- After each section, close book
- Answer your questions out loud
- Put in your own words

**Review:**
- Review all questions
- Check answers
- Connect sections

### 2. Annotating

**What to Mark:**
- ✅ Main ideas
- ✅ Key terms
- ✅ Important details
- ✅ Examples
- ✅ Surprising information
- ❌ Don't mark everything

**Annotation Symbols:**
- ✓ - Agree/Important
- ✗ - Disagree
- ? - Question/Unclear
- ! - Important/Surprising
- → - Leads to/connects
- * - Good example

**Margin Notes:**
- Summarize paragraph in 5 words
- Write questions
- Make connections
- Add examples

**What:** Voice your thinking as you read

**Ask:**

- "I don't understand..."
- "This means..."

## Comprehension Monitoring

### How to Check Understanding

**Ask Yourself:**
- Can I summarize what I just read?
- Do I know the main idea?
- Can I explain this to someone else?
- {{UNDERSTANDING_CHECK}}

**If Confused:**
1. **Stop** - Don't keep reading
2. **Reread** - Read again slowly
3. **Look Back** - Check earlier sections
4. **Look Ahead** - Check what's coming
5. **Break It Down** - Read sentence by sentence
6. **Ask for Help** - Look up or ask someone

### Fix-Up Strategies

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Don't understand word | Look up, use context clues |
| Lost track | Reread last paragraph |
| Confusing sentence | Break into smaller parts |
| Too much info | Focus on main idea |
| Getting bored | Take break, change location |
| Falling asleep | Stand up, drink water |

## Speed Reading Techniques

### Chunking

**What:** Read groups of words, not single words

**Practice:**
Normal: "The | quick | brown | fox"
Chunked: "The quick | brown fox"

**Start:**
- 2 words at a time
- Then 3
- Work up to phrases

### Pointer Method

**What:** Use finger/pen to guide eyes

**How:**
- Move finger smoothly under text
- Eyes follow finger
- Start slow, speed up
- {{POINTER_SPEED}} words per minute

### Stop Subvocalizing

**What:** Stop "saying" words in your head

**How:**
- Chew gum while reading
- Hum quietly
- Focus on meaning, not sound
- {{SUBVOCAL_PROGRESS}}

### Expand Eye Span

**Practice:**
- Read from margin to margin
- Don't move head
- Use peripheral vision
- {{EYE_SPAN_PRACTICE}}

## Critical Reading

### Analyzing Arguments

**Check For:**
- Main claim/thesis
- Supporting evidence
- Logical reasoning
- Counterarguments
- Author's bias

**Questions:**
- What is the author's purpose?
- What evidence supports the claim?
- Is the logic sound?
- Are there counterarguments?
- Is the source credible?
