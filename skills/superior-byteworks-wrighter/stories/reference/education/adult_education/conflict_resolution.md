## Conflict Styles

### The Five Styles

**1. Competing (Win-Lose):**
- "I win, you lose"
- Assertive, uncooperative
- Good for: emergencies, when you're right
- Bad for: relationships, team morale
- {{COMPETE_EXAMPLE}}

**2. Collaborating (Win-Win):**
- "We both win"
- Assertive, cooperative
- Good for: important issues, relationships
- Bad for: simple decisions, time pressure
- {{COLLABORATE_EXAMPLE}}

**3. Compromising (Middle Ground):**
- "We both give a little"
- Moderate assertiveness, moderate cooperation
- Good for: time pressure, equal power
- Bad for: win-win possible, principle issues
- {{COMPROMISE_EXAMPLE}}

**4. Avoiding (Lose-Lose):**
- "I don't deal with it"
- Unassertive, uncooperative
- Good for: trivial issues, danger
- Bad for: important issues, resentment
- {{AVOID_EXAMPLE}}

**5. Accommodating (Lose-Win):**
- "You win, I lose"
- Unassertive, cooperative
- Good for: relationship more important
- Bad for: your needs matter, taken advantage of
- {{ACCOMMODATE_EXAMPLE}}

### The DESC Method

**D - Describe (Facts only):**
"When [specific behavior] happens..."
- No judgments
- No labels
- Just observable facts
- {{DESCRIBE_FACTS}}

**E - Express (Your feelings):**
"I feel [emotion]..."
- Use "I" statements
- Own your feelings
- "I feel frustrated" not "You frustrate me"
- {{EXPRESS_FEELINGS}}

**S - Specify (What you want):**
"I would like [specific behavior]..."
- Be clear and specific
- Ask for something doable
- {{SPECIFY_WANT}}

**C - Consequences (Outcome):**
"This would help because..."
- Positive outcome
- Mutual benefit
- {{CONSEQUENCES}}

**Full Example:**
"When meetings start late (Describe), I feel frustrated (Express). I would like us to start on time (Specify). This would help us finish earlier and respect everyone's time (Consequences)."

## Resolution Strategies

### Strategy 1: Pause and Cool Down

**When:** Emotions are high
**How:**
- Say "I need a break"
- Agree to come back
- Cool down (walk, breathe)
- Return calm
- {{COOL_DOWN_PLAN}}

### Strategy 2: Focus on Interests, Not Positions

**Positions:** What they say they want
**Interests:** Why they want it

**Example:**
- Position: "I want a raise"
- Interest: "I need to feel valued" or "I need more money for family"
