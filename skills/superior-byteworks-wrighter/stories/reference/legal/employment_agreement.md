## Recitals

**WHEREAS**, [COMPANY NAME], a [TYPE OF ENTITY] organized and existing under the laws of the State of [STATE] (the "Employer"), desires to employ [EMPLOYEE NAME] (the "Employee") upon the terms and conditions set forth herein;

**WHEREAS**, the Employee desires to accept such employment and to perform the duties and responsibilities assigned by the Employer;

**WHEREAS**, the Employer and the Employee (each a "Party" and collectively the "Parties") desire to set forth the terms and conditions of the Employee's employment, including compensation, benefits, obligations, and post-employment restrictions;

**WHEREAS**, the Employee acknowledges that the Employer will provide access to confidential and proprietary information, trade secrets, and specialized training as a condition of employment;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and conditions set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

## Article II — Position and Duties

### Section 2.1 — Position

The Employer hereby employs the Employee in the position of **[JOB TITLE]**, reporting to **[SUPERVISOR NAME AND TITLE]**, within the **[DEPARTMENT NAME]** department.

### Section 2.2 — Duties and Responsibilities

The Employee shall perform the following duties and responsibilities, as well as such other duties as may be reasonably assigned by the Employer from time to time:

- (a) [PRIMARY DUTY/RESPONSIBILITY];
- (b) [SECONDARY DUTY/RESPONSIBILITY];
- (c) [ADDITIONAL DUTY/RESPONSIBILITY];
- (d) Such other duties consistent with the Employee's position as may be assigned by the Employer.

### Section 2.3 — Work Location

The Employee's primary work location shall be [OFFICE ADDRESS / REMOTE / HYBRID]. The Employer reserves the right to modify the work location with reasonable notice, subject to applicable law.

### Section 2.4 — Work Schedule

The Employee shall work [FULL-TIME: minimum 40 hours per week / PART-TIME: [X] hours per week]. The standard work schedule is [DAYS AND HOURS], subject to modification by the Employer.

### Section 2.5 — Devotion of Time

The Employee shall devote their full professional time, attention, skill, and energy to the performance of their duties. The Employee shall not engage in any other employment, consulting, or business activity without the Employer's prior written consent.

## Article IV — Benefits

### Section 4.1 — Health and Welfare Benefits

The Employee shall be eligible to participate in the Employer's benefits programs, subject to eligibility requirements and plan terms:

- (a) Health insurance (medical, dental, vision);
- (b) Life insurance and accidental death & dismemberment (AD&D);
- (c) Short-term and long-term disability insurance;
- (d) Employee assistance program (EAP);
- (e) Flexible spending account (FSA) or health savings account (HSA).

### Section 4.2 — Retirement Benefits

## Article V — Confidentiality

### Section 5.1 — Definition

"Confidential Information" includes all non-public information, trade secrets, and proprietary data of the Employer, including but not limited to: technical information, business strategies, customer lists, financial data, product plans, source code, algorithms, and employee information.

### Section 5.2 — Non-Disclosure Obligations

The Employee agrees to: (a) hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence; (b) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written authorization; (c) use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing duties under this Agreement; (d) take all reasonable measures to prevent unauthorized disclosure.

### Section 5.3 — Duration

The obligations under this Article shall survive the termination of this Agreement and shall continue indefinitely with respect to trade secrets and for a period of [X] years with respect to other Confidential Information.

### Section 5.4 — Whistleblower Protections

Nothing in this Article shall prohibit the Employee from: (a) reporting suspected violations of law to governmental authorities; (b) cooperating with government investigations; or (c) making disclosures protected under whistleblower statutes, including under the Defend Trade Secrets Act.

---

## Article VI — Intellectual Property

### Section 6.1 — Work Product Assignment

The Employee hereby assigns to the Employer all right, title, and interest in any and all inventions, discoveries, works of authorship, software, designs, and other intellectual property created by the Employee during the course of employment and relating to the Employer's business ("Work Product").

### Section 6.2 — Prior Inventions

The Employee has disclosed on **Exhibit A** all inventions and intellectual property owned by the Employee prior to the date of this Agreement that relate to the Employer's business ("Prior Inventions"). Any invention not listed on Exhibit A shall be presumed to be Work Product.

### Section 6.3 — Moral Rights Waiver

To the extent permitted by applicable law, the Employee waives all moral rights in Work Product, including rights of attribution and integrity.

### Section 6.4 — Cooperation

The Employee shall cooperate with the Employer in securing intellectual property protections for Work Product, including executing patent applications, copyright registrations, and related documents, both during and after employment.

---

## Article VII — Restrictive Covenants

### Section 7.1 — Non-Competition

During the term of employment and for a period of **[X] months** following termination (the "Restricted Period"), the Employee shall not directly or indirectly engage in, own, manage, or participate in any business that competes with the Employer within [GEOGRAPHIC AREA].

### Section 7.2 — Non-Solicitation of Employees

During the Restricted Period, the Employee shall not directly or indirectly solicit, recruit, or induce any employee or contractor of the Employer to leave their engagement with the Employer.

### Section 7.3 — Non-Solicitation of Clients

During the Restricted Period, the Employee shall not directly or indirectly solicit, divert, or accept business from any client or customer of the Employer with whom the Employee had material contact during the last twelve (12) months of employment.

### Section 7.4 — Enforceability

The Parties acknowledge that the restrictions in this Article are reasonable and necessary to protect the Employer's legitimate business interests. If any restriction is found to be overbroad, the Parties agree that the court may modify the restriction to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable.

---

## Article VIII — Termination

### Section 8.1 — At-Will Employment

Unless otherwise required by applicable law, employment under this Agreement is "at will," meaning either Party may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause or notice.

### Section 8.2 — Termination by Employer for Cause

The Employer may terminate the Employee's employment immediately for "Cause," which includes: (a) material breach of this Agreement; (b) conviction of a felony or crime of moral turpitude; (c) fraud, dishonesty, or willful misconduct; (d) failure to perform duties after written notice and reasonable opportunity to cure; (e) violation of Employer policies.

### Section 8.3 — Termination Without Cause

If the Employer terminates the Employee's employment without Cause, the Employee shall be entitled to: (a) accrued but unpaid Base Salary through the date of termination; (b) severance pay equal to [X] months of Base Salary; (c) continuation of health benefits for [X] months (COBRA subsidy); (d) accelerated vesting of [X]% of unvested equity, subject to execution of a separation agreement and general release.

### Section 8.4 — Resignation

The Employee may resign at any time by providing [X] weeks' prior written notice. The Employer may, at its discretion, waive the notice period and accelerate the termination date.

### Section 8.5 — Return of Property

Upon termination, the Employee shall immediately return all Employer property, including equipment, documents, keys, access credentials, and Confidential Information in any form.

## Article IX — Representations and Warranties

### Section 9.1 — Employee Representations

The Employee represents and warrants that: (a) they are legally authorized to work in [COUNTRY]; (b) they are not bound by any agreement that would prevent performance of duties under this Agreement; (c) all information provided during the hiring process is truthful and accurate; (d) they will comply with all applicable laws and Employer policies.

### Section 9.2 — Employer Representations

The Employer represents and warrants that: (a) it has the authority to enter into this Agreement; (b) this Agreement has been duly authorized; and (c) the terms of employment comply with applicable employment laws.

## Article X — General Provisions

### Section 10.1 — Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of [STATE], without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

### Section 10.2 — Dispute Resolution

Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be resolved through [mandatory mediation followed by binding arbitration under the rules of the American Arbitration Association / litigation in the state or federal courts of [COUNTY, STATE]].

### Section 10.3 — Entire Agreement

This Agreement, together with any exhibits and schedules attached hereto, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties regarding the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior agreements, representations, and understandings.

### Section 10.4 — Amendment

This Agreement may not be amended or modified except by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

### Section 10.5 — Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

### Section 10.6 — Notices

All notices shall be in writing and delivered to the addresses set forth in Article I.

### Section 10.7 — Counterparts

This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original. Electronic signatures shall be deemed valid and binding.
