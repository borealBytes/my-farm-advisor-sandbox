## Recitals

**WHEREAS**, the undersigned individuals and/or entities (each a "Partner" and collectively the "Partners") desire to form a partnership under the laws of the State of [STATE] for the purpose of conducting the business described herein;

**WHEREAS**, the Partners desire to set forth the terms and conditions governing the formation, management, operation, and dissolution of the partnership (the "Partnership");

**WHEREAS**, the Partners intend to define their respective rights, obligations, capital contributions, profit and loss allocations, and decision-making authority;

**WHEREAS**, the Partners acknowledge that a clear and comprehensive partnership agreement is essential to the orderly conduct of the Partnership's business and the protection of each Partner's interests;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants, promises, and agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Partners agree as follows:

---

## Article I — Formation and Name

### Section 1.1 — Formation

The Partners hereby form a [General Partnership / Limited Partnership / Limited Liability Partnership] (the "Partnership") pursuant to the [Uniform Partnership Act / Revised Uniform Limited Partnership Act / applicable state statute] of the State of [STATE].

### Section 1.2 — Partnership Name

The Partnership shall conduct business under the name **"[PARTNERSHIP NAME]"** and may use such trade names or "doing business as" names as the Partners may approve from time to time.

### Section 1.3 — Principal Office

The principal office of the Partnership shall be located at [BUSINESS ADDRESS]. The Partners may change the principal office upon unanimous written consent.

### Section 1.4 — Registered Agent

The registered agent for service of process shall be [AGENT NAME] at [AGENT ADDRESS].

---

## Article II — Purpose and Term

### Section 2.1 — Purpose

The purpose of the Partnership is to engage in the business of [DESCRIBE BUSINESS PURPOSE], and any other lawful business activities as the Partners may agree upon from time to time.

### Section 2.2 — Term

The Partnership shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue until dissolved in accordance with Article XI of this Agreement.

---

## Article III — Partners

### Section 3.1 — Identification of Partners

| Partner Name     | Type      | Ownership (%) | Initial Contribution |
| ---------------- | --------- | ------------- | -------------------- |
| [PARTNER 1 NAME] | [General] | [X]%          | $[AMOUNT]            |
| [PARTNER 2 NAME] | [General] | [X]%          | $[AMOUNT]            |
| [PARTNER 3 NAME] | [Limited] | [X]%          | $[AMOUNT]            |

### Section 3.2 — Admission of New Partners

No new Partner shall be admitted to the Partnership without the unanimous written consent of all existing Partners. Any new Partner shall execute a written instrument agreeing to be bound by the terms of this Agreement.

### Section 3.3 — Representations and Warranties

Each Partner represents and warrants that: (a) they have the legal capacity and authority to enter into this Agreement; (b) this Agreement does not conflict with any other agreement to which they are a party; and (c) they are entering into this Agreement voluntarily and with full knowledge of its terms.

---

## Article IV — Capital Contributions

### Section 4.1 — Initial Contributions

Each Partner shall make an initial capital contribution in the amount set forth in Section 3.1 within [X] days of the Effective Date. Contributions may consist of cash, property, or services as agreed upon by the Partners.

### Section 4.2 — Additional Contributions

No Partner shall be required to make additional capital contributions without their written consent. If additional contributions are needed, the Partners shall determine the amounts, terms, and conditions by [unanimous / majority] vote.

### Section 4.3 — Capital Accounts

The Partnership shall maintain a separate capital account for each Partner. Capital accounts shall be adjusted in accordance with applicable tax regulations and generally accepted accounting principles.

### Section 4.4 — No Interest on Capital

No Partner shall be entitled to interest on their capital contributions or capital account balance unless otherwise agreed upon in writing by all Partners.

### Section 4.5 — Return of Capital

No Partner shall have the right to withdraw or demand the return of their capital contribution except upon dissolution of the Partnership or as otherwise provided in this Agreement.

---

## Article V — Allocation of Profits and Losses

### Section 5.1 — Profit and Loss Allocation

Net profits and net losses of the Partnership shall be allocated among the Partners in proportion to their respective ownership percentages as set forth in Section 3.1, unless otherwise agreed upon in writing.

### Section 5.2 — Distributions

Distributions of available cash shall be made at such times and in such amounts as determined by the [Managing Partner / unanimous vote of the Partners], but no less frequently than [quarterly / annually]. Distributions shall be made in proportion to each Partner's ownership percentage.

### Section 5.3 — Tax Distributions

The Partnership shall make tax distributions to each Partner in an amount sufficient to cover the estimated income tax liability arising from the Partner's allocable share of Partnership income, calculated at the highest applicable marginal tax rate.

### Section 5.4 — Guaranteed Payments

The following Partners shall receive guaranteed payments for services rendered to the Partnership:

| [PARTNER NAME] | $[AMOUNT]                 |
| [PARTNER NAME] | $[AMOUNT]                 |

## Article VI — Management and Decision-Making

### Section 6.1 — Management Authority

The day-to-day management of the Partnership shall be vested in [all Partners jointly / the Managing Partner, [NAME]]. The Managing Partner shall have authority to: (a) execute contracts and agreements in the ordinary course of business; (b) hire and terminate employees; (c) manage the Partnership's bank accounts; and (d) take such other actions as are necessary for the ordinary operation of the business.

### Section 6.2 — Major Decisions

The following decisions require the [unanimous / supermajority (X%)] written consent of the Partners:

- (a) Admission or removal of a Partner;
- (b) Amendment of this Agreement;
- (c) Sale or disposition of all or substantially all Partnership assets;
- (d) Incurrence of indebtedness exceeding $[THRESHOLD];
- (e) Entry into contracts exceeding $[THRESHOLD] or [X] years in duration;
- (f) Change in the nature of the Partnership's business;
- (g) Merger, consolidation, or dissolution of the Partnership;
- (h) Filing for bankruptcy or insolvency proceedings.

### Section 6.3 — Voting

Each Partner shall have voting rights proportional to their ownership percentage. Decisions not classified as Major Decisions under Section 6.2 may be made by a simple majority vote.

### Section 6.4 — Meetings

Partners shall hold regular meetings at least [quarterly / monthly]. Special meetings may be called by any Partner upon [X] days' written notice. Meetings may be held in person, by telephone, or by video conference.

### Section 6.5 — Books and Records

The Partnership shall maintain complete and accurate books of account, financial records, and minutes of Partner meetings at the principal office. Each Partner shall have the right to inspect and copy such records during normal business hours.

---

## Article VII — Partner Duties and Restrictions

### Section 7.1 — Fiduciary Duties

Each Partner owes fiduciary duties to the Partnership and the other Partners, including the duties of loyalty, care, and good faith. Partners shall act in the best interests of the Partnership and shall not engage in self-dealing or usurp Partnership opportunities.

### Section 7.2 — Devotion of Time

Each Partner shall devote such time and attention to the business of the Partnership as is reasonably necessary for the conduct of the Partnership's affairs.

### Section 7.3 — Competing Activities

No Partner shall engage in any business activity that directly competes with the Partnership without the prior written consent of all other Partners.

### Section 7.4 — Confidentiality

Each Partner shall maintain the confidentiality of all non-public information relating to the Partnership's business, financial condition, clients, and trade secrets, both during the term of the Partnership and for a period of [X] years following a Partner's withdrawal or the dissolution of the Partnership.

---

## Article VIII — Transfer of Partnership Interests

### Section 8.1 — Restrictions on Transfer

No Partner may sell, assign, pledge, or otherwise transfer their Partnership interest, in whole or in part, without the prior written consent of all other Partners.

### Section 8.2 — Right of First Refusal

If a Partner (the "Selling Partner") desires to transfer their Partnership interest, the remaining Partners shall have a right of first refusal to purchase such interest on the same terms and conditions offered by any bona fide third-party purchaser. The remaining Partners shall have [X] days from receipt of written notice to exercise this right.

### Section 8.3 — Permitted Transfers

Notwithstanding Section 8.1, a Partner may transfer their interest to: (a) a revocable living trust for the benefit of the Partner or their family members; or (b) an entity wholly owned and controlled by the Partner, provided that the transferee agrees in writing to be bound by the terms of this Agreement.

### Section 8.4 — Valuation

For purposes of any transfer under this Article, the fair market value of a Partnership interest shall be determined by [mutual agreement of the Partners / an independent appraiser selected by the Partners / the formula set forth in Exhibit A].

---

## Article IX — Withdrawal and Removal of Partners

### Section 9.1 — Voluntary Withdrawal

A Partner may voluntarily withdraw from the Partnership upon [X] months' prior written notice to all other Partners. The withdrawing Partner shall be entitled to receive the fair market value of their Partnership interest, payable in [a lump sum / installments over X months].

### Section 9.2 — Involuntary Removal

A Partner may be removed from the Partnership upon the [unanimous / supermajority] vote of the remaining Partners for cause, including: (a) material breach of this Agreement; (b) conviction of a felony; (c) fraud or willful misconduct; (d) bankruptcy or insolvency; or (e) permanent disability rendering the Partner unable to perform their duties.

### Section 9.3 — Effect of Withdrawal or Removal

Upon withdrawal or removal, the departing Partner's interest shall be purchased by the Partnership or the remaining Partners at fair market value, and the departing Partner shall have no further rights or obligations under this Agreement except as provided in Article VII, Section 7.4.

---

## Article X — Indemnification and Liability

### Section 10.1 — Indemnification

The Partnership shall indemnify and hold harmless each Partner from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to the Partner's good-faith actions on behalf of the Partnership, except to the extent caused by the Partner's gross negligence, willful misconduct, or breach of this Agreement.

### Section 10.2 — Limitation of Liability

No Partner shall be personally liable for the debts, obligations, or liabilities of the Partnership beyond their capital contribution, except as required by applicable law for general partners.

### Section 10.3 — Insurance

The Partnership shall maintain such insurance coverage as the Partners deem reasonably necessary, including general liability, professional liability, and property insurance.

---

## Article XI — Dissolution and Winding Up

### Section 11.1 — Events of Dissolution

The Partnership shall be dissolved upon the occurrence of any of the following: (a) the unanimous written consent of all Partners; (b) the occurrence of any event that makes it unlawful for the Partnership to continue; (c) the entry of a judicial decree of dissolution; or (d) the bankruptcy or dissolution of the Partnership.

### Section 11.2 — Winding Up

Upon dissolution, the Partnership's affairs shall be wound up by the [Managing Partner / a liquidating partner designated by majority vote]. The winding-up process shall include: (a) completing unfinished business; (b) collecting outstanding receivables; (c) paying debts and obligations; and (d) distributing remaining assets to the Partners.

### Section 11.3 — Order of Distribution

Upon dissolution, Partnership assets shall be distributed in the following order of priority: (a) payment of debts and obligations to creditors; (b) repayment of outstanding loans from Partners; (c) return of capital contributions; and (d) distribution of remaining assets in proportion to ownership percentages.

---

## Article XII — General Provisions

### Section 12.1 — Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of [STATE], without regard to conflict of laws principles.

### Section 12.2 — Dispute Resolution

Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall first be submitted to mediation. If mediation is unsuccessful, the dispute shall be resolved by binding arbitration under the rules of the [American Arbitration Association / JAMS], conducted in [CITY, STATE].

### Section 12.3 — Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement among the Partners and supersedes all prior agreements, representations, and understandings relating to the Partnership.

### Section 12.4 — Amendment

This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by all Partners.

### Section 12.5 — Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

### Section 12.6 — Notices

All notices shall be in writing and delivered to the addresses maintained in the Partnership's records.

### Section 12.7 — Counterparts

This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original. Electronic signatures shall be deemed valid and binding.
