## 1. Principal Risk Factors

### 1.1 Market Risk

The value of the Fund's investments may decline due to general market conditions, economic trends, or events that are not specifically related to the issuer of a security. Equity and fixed income markets may experience significant volatility, which may adversely affect the Fund's NAV.

### 1.2 Index Tracking Risk

The Fund may not perfectly replicate the performance of its benchmark index. Factors contributing to tracking error include:

- Fund expenses and fees
- Transaction costs from portfolio rebalancing
- Cash drag from uninvested cash
- Differences in timing of NAV calculation vs. index calculation
- Corporate actions and index reconstitutions

### 1.3 ETF Trading Risk

- **Premium/Discount Risk**: ETF shares may trade at a price above (premium) or below (discount) the Fund's NAV
- **Bid-Ask Spread Risk**: The difference between buying and selling prices represents a cost to investors
- **Trading Halt Risk**: Exchange trading may be halted under certain market conditions

### 1.4 Liquidity Risk

Some of the Fund's holdings may be difficult to sell at favorable prices, particularly during periods of market stress. This may affect the Fund's ability to meet redemption requests or rebalance its portfolio.
