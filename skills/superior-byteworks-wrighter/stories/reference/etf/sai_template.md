## Taxation of the Fund

### Tax Status

The Fund intends to elect and qualify each year for treatment as a "regulated investment company" ("RIC") under Subchapter M of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (the "Code"). To qualify as a RIC, the Fund must satisfy certain requirements regarding the source of its income, the diversification of its assets, and the distribution of its income:

**Income Test**: At least 90% of the Fund's gross income must be derived from dividends, interest, payments with respect to certain securities loans, gains from the sale of stock or securities, or other income derived from the Fund's business of investing.

**Diversification Test**: At each quarter end:

- At least 50% of the Fund's total assets must be invested in (a) cash and cash items, (b) U.S. government securities, (c) securities of other RICs, and (d) other securities, with each issuer limited to 5% of total assets and 10% of the issuer's outstanding voting securities.
- No more than 25% of the Fund's total assets may be invested in the securities of any one issuer (other than U.S. government securities or RICs).

**Distribution Requirement**: The Fund must distribute at least 90% of its investment company taxable income and net tax-exempt interest income each year.

### Excise Tax

The Fund intends to distribute sufficient amounts to avoid the 4% federal excise tax imposed on RICs that do not distribute at least 98% of their ordinary income for the calendar year and 98.2% of their capital gain net income for the one-year period ending October 31.

### Distributions to Shareholders

Distributions of the Fund's investment company taxable income (other than qualified dividend income and net capital gain) will generally be taxable to shareholders as ordinary income. Distributions of net capital gain will be taxable to shareholders as long-term capital gain, regardless of how long a shareholder has held Shares.

**Qualified Dividend Income**: Distributions of qualified dividend income to individual shareholders may be taxed at reduced rates, provided certain holding period and other requirements are met.

### Creation and Redemption Tax Considerations

The Fund may make in-kind distributions of portfolio securities in connection with the redemption of Creation Units. These in-kind transfers generally will not be taxable events for the Fund, which may help the Fund minimize capital gains distributions compared to traditional mutual funds.

## Calculation of Performance Data

### Total Return

Average annual total return is calculated by determining the ending redeemable value of a hypothetical $1,000 investment at the beginning of the relevant period, assuming reinvestment of all dividends and distributions, and dividing by the initial investment, in accordance with the following formula:

**P(1+T)^n = ERV**

Where:

- P = hypothetical initial payment of $1,000
- T = average annual total return
- n = number of years
- ERV = ending redeemable value at end of period

### After-Tax Returns

After-tax returns are calculated using the highest individual federal marginal income tax rates in effect during the relevant period and do not reflect the impact of state and local taxes. The two after-tax return figures are:

1. **Return After Taxes on Distributions**: Reflects the impact of taxes on distributions only
2. **Return After Taxes on Distributions and Sale of Shares**: Reflects the impact of taxes on both distributions and the sale of Shares
