## IV. The ETF Structure

### A. Overview of Operations

The Fund will operate as an ETF. Shares of the Fund will be listed and traded on the Exchange. The Fund will issue and redeem Shares only in aggregations of a specified number of Shares known as "Creation Units" (each consisting of {{CREATION_UNIT_SIZE}} Shares) and only with Authorized Participants ("APs").

Individual Shares may be purchased and sold throughout the trading day on the Exchange at market-determined prices through broker-dealers. The price of Shares trading on the Exchange may differ from the Fund's NAV (i.e., Shares may trade at a premium or discount to NAV).

### B. Creation and Redemption

1. **Creation Process**: An AP may purchase Creation Units from the Fund by delivering to the Fund a designated portfolio of securities ("Deposit Securities") and a specified cash amount ("Cash Component"), as published by the Fund each Business Day through the National Securities Clearing Corporation ("NSCC").

2. **Redemption Process**: An AP may redeem Creation Units by tendering them to the Fund in exchange for a designated portfolio of securities ("Redemption Securities") and a Cash Component.

3. **Transaction Fees**: A Transaction Fee will be imposed to offset the Fund's transfer and other costs associated with the issuance and redemption of Creation Units. The Transaction Fee will be limited to amounts disclosed in the Fund's prospectus and SAI.

## V. Legal Analysis

### A. Sections 2(a)(32) and 5(a)(1) — Definition of Redeemable Security and Open-End Company

Section 2(a)(32) of the 1940 Act defines a "redeemable security" as any security, other than short-term paper, under the terms of which the holder, upon its presentation to the issuer, is entitled to receive approximately his proportionate share of the issuer's current net assets.

The Fund's Shares will be redeemable in Creation Units only. Individual shareholders will not have the right to redeem Shares directly from the Fund. Applicants submit that the requested relief is appropriate because:

1. The ETF structure provides shareholders with a practical and efficient means of disposing of their Shares through secondary market transactions.
2. The arbitrage mechanism inherent in the ETF structure is designed to minimize deviations between the market price of Shares and NAV.
3. Authorized Participants maintain the ability to create and redeem Creation Units, providing a direct redemption mechanism.

### B. Section 22(d) and Rule 22c-1 — Secondary Market Trading

Section 22(d) of the 1940 Act prohibits a dealer from selling a redeemable security except at a price described in the current prospectus. Rule 22c-1 requires that redeemable securities be sold and redeemed at a price based on NAV next computed after receipt of a tender.

Applicants submit that the concerns underlying Section 22(d) and Rule 22c-1 are adequately addressed because:

1. Shares will be listed and traded on the Exchange, subject to Exchange rules designed to protect investors.
2. The Fund will provide a high degree of transparency through {{TRANSPARENCY_MECHANISM}}.
3. The arbitrage mechanism will keep market prices closely aligned with NAV.
4. Investors will have access to real-time pricing information, including {{IOPV / VIIV / NAV_PROXY}} disseminated at least every {{INTERVAL}} seconds during trading hours.

### C. Section 22(e) — Suspension of Redemption

Section 22(e) of the 1940 Act generally prohibits a registered investment company from suspending the right of redemption or postponing the date of payment for more than seven days after tender.

The Fund will satisfy redemption requests within the time periods required by Section 22(e), subject to the standard exceptions set forth therein. In-kind redemptions will settle in accordance with standard securities settlement cycles (currently T+{{SETTLEMENT_DAYS}}).

### D. Section 17(a) — Affiliated Transactions

Sections 17(a)(1) and 17(a)(2) of the 1940 Act generally prohibit an affiliated person of a registered investment company from selling securities to, or purchasing securities from, the company.

The in-kind creation and redemption process may involve transactions between the Fund and persons who are affiliated persons, or second-tier affiliates, of the Fund. Applicants submit that the requested relief under Section 17(b) is warranted because:

1. The terms of in-kind transactions are based on the objective, verifiable standard of the Fund's NAV.
2. The transactions are consistent with the policies of the Fund.
3. The transactions are consistent with the general purposes of the 1940 Act.
