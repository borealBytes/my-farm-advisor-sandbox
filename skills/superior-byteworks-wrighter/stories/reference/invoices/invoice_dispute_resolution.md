## 1. Purpose

This document establishes the standard process for receiving, investigating, and resolving customer billing disputes in a fair, timely, and well-documented manner. It ensures that disputes are handled consistently, customer relationships are preserved, and financial records remain accurate.

---

## 2. Scope

This process applies to:

- All disputes related to invoices issued by [Company Name]
- Disputes regarding pricing, quantities, service descriptions, taxes, or terms
- Disputes raised by customers, their agents, or authorized representatives

**Exclusions:** Warranty claims, product return/refund requests, and service-level agreement (SLA) credits are governed by separate processes. However, if such claims result in a billing adjustment, this process governs the invoice-side resolution.

---

## 3. Definitions

| Term                   | Definition                                                                   |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Dispute**            | A formal objection by a customer to all or part of an invoiced amount        |
| **Disputant**          | The customer or authorized representative raising the dispute                |
| **Disputed Amount**    | The specific dollar amount being contested                                   |
| **Undisputed Amount**  | The portion of the invoice not under dispute; remains due per original terms |
| **Credit Memo**        | A document reducing the customer's outstanding balance                       |
| **Debit Memo**         | A document increasing the customer's outstanding balance                     |
| **Good Faith Dispute** | A dispute supported by reasonable evidence or contractual basis              |
| **Frivolous Dispute**  | A dispute lacking factual or contractual basis, raised to delay payment      |

## 4. Dispute Submission Requirements

### 4.1 How to Submit a Dispute

Customers must submit disputes in writing using one of the following channels:

| Channel            | Contact                                           |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------- |
| **Email**          | [disputes@company.com]                            |
| **Billing Portal** | [https://billing.company.com/disputes]            |
| **Mail**           | [Company Name], Attn: Billing Disputes, [Address] |
| **Fax**            | [(XXX) XXX-XXXX]                                  |

### 4.2 Required Information

All dispute submissions must include:

| Required Field                   | Description                                             |
| -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Customer name and account number | Legal entity name and [ACCT-XXXXXX]                     |
| Invoice number(s)                | Specific invoice(s) being disputed                      |
| Disputed amount                  | Dollar amount contested                                 |
| Dispute category                 | Pricing, quantity, service, tax, or terms               |
| Detailed explanation             | Clear description of the discrepancy                    |
| Supporting documentation         | Contract excerpts, emails, POs, delivery records        |
| Requested resolution             | Credit, revised invoice, or other specific remedy       |
| Contact person                   | Name, title, phone, and email for dispute communication |

### 4.3 Submission Deadline

Disputes must be submitted within **15 business days** of the invoice date. Disputes submitted after this period may still be accepted at management discretion but do not suspend late fee accrual on the disputed amount.

## 5. Dispute Workflow

### 5.1 Process Steps

| Step | Action                              | Owner              | SLA        | Deliverable                              |
| ---- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------ | ---------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| 1    | Receive dispute notification        | AR Team            | Day 0      | Dispute logged                           |
| 2    | Validate completeness of submission | AR Specialist      | Day 1      | Complete/incomplete determination        |
| 3    | Assign dispute tracking number      | Billing System     | Day 1      | Dispute ID (DSP-[YYYY]-[NNNNN])          |
| 4    | Send acknowledgment to customer     | AR Specialist      | Day 1      | Acknowledgment email/letter              |
| 5    | Research and gather documentation   | AR Specialist      | Days 2–5   | Investigation file                       |
| 6    | Consult with internal stakeholders  | AR Specialist      | Days 3–5   | Stakeholder input documented             |
| 7    | Determine dispute validity          | AR Manager         | Day 6      | Validity determination                   |
| 8    | Propose resolution to customer      | AR Specialist      | Day 7      | Resolution proposal                      |
| 9    | Customer accepts or counters        | Customer           | Days 7–12  | Customer response                        |
| 10   | Implement agreed resolution         | AR/Finance         | Days 12–14 | Credit memo, revised invoice, or closure |
| 11   | Update financial records            | Revenue Accounting | Day 14     | GL adjustments posted                    |
| 12   | Close dispute and archive           | AR Manager         | Day 15     | Dispute closed in system                 |

### 5.2 Incomplete Submissions

If a dispute submission is incomplete:

1. AR Specialist notifies the customer within 1 business day, specifying missing information
2. Customer has 5 business days to provide missing items
3. The SLA clock pauses until complete information is received
4. If the customer does not respond within 5 business days, the dispute is closed as "Withdrawn — Incomplete"

## 6. Investigation Procedures

### 6.1 Internal Research

The AR Specialist must review the following during investigation:

- [ ] Original customer contract and amendments
- [ ] Statement of work or service order
- [ ] Rate schedule and pricing terms
- [ ] Usage/consumption data from the billing system
- [ ] Delivery confirmations or proof of service
- [ ] Purchase order and PO remaining balance
- [ ] Previous correspondence regarding the account
- [ ] Tax exemption certificates on file
- [ ] Prior dispute history for pattern analysis

### 6.2 Stakeholder Consultation

| Dispute Category            | Stakeholder(s) to Consult            |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Pricing or rate discrepancy | Sales Operations, Deal Desk          |
| Quantity or usage dispute   | Service Delivery, Operations         |
| Service description error   | Project Management, Customer Success |
| Tax calculation issue       | Tax/Compliance, External tax advisor |
| Contract terms dispute      | Legal, Sales Operations              |
| Duplicate billing           | AR Team (internal review)            |

### 6.3 Documentation Standards

All investigation activities must be documented in the dispute tracking system, including:

- Date and time of each action
- Name of the person performing the action
- Summary of findings at each step
- Copies of all supporting documents
- Records of all customer communications

## 7. Resolution Types

### 7.1 Resolution Categories

| Resolution Type     | Description                                               | Financial Impact       | Approval Required    |
| ------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------- | -------------------- |
| **Full Credit**     | Entire disputed amount credited                           | Credit memo issued     | Per approval matrix  |
| **Partial Credit**  | Portion of disputed amount credited                       | Credit memo issued     | Per approval matrix  |
| **Revised Invoice** | Original voided; corrected invoice issued                 | Net zero or adjustment | AR Manager           |
| **No Adjustment**   | Dispute determined to be invalid                          | None                   | AR Manager           |
| **Payment Plan**    | Balance valid but customer requests terms                 | Deferred collection    | Director of Finance  |
| **Write-Off**       | Balance deemed uncollectable                              | Bad debt expense       | Per write-off policy |
| **Goodwill Credit** | Credit issued to preserve relationship (no billing error) | Credit memo issued     | Director of Finance  |

### 7.2 Approval Matrix

| Credit Amount    | Approval Authority  |
| ---------------- | ------------------- |
| Up to $1,000     | AR Manager          |
| $1,001–$5,000    | Director of Finance |
| $5,001–$25,000   | CFO/Controller      |
| $25,001–$100,000 | CFO + VP of Sales   |
| Over $100,000    | CFO + CEO           |

### 7.3 Goodwill Credits

Goodwill credits are issued when no billing error exists but a credit is warranted for relationship or retention purposes. These require:

- Written justification from the Sales Account Manager or Customer Success Manager
- Approval from the Director of Finance (regardless of amount)
- Documentation that the credit is non-precedential
- Notation in the customer file for future reference

## 8. Late Fee Treatment During Disputes

### 8.1 Fee Suspension Rules

| Dispute Status                       | Late Fee Treatment                                  |
| ------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Good faith dispute submitted on time | Late fees suspended on disputed amount              |
| Dispute submitted after deadline     | Late fees continue to accrue                        |
| Undisputed portion of invoice        | Late fees accrue per standard terms                 |
| Dispute resolved — credit issued     | Accrued late fees on credited amount reversed       |
| Dispute resolved — no adjustment     | Accrued late fees reinstated from original due date |
| Frivolous dispute determination      | Late fees accrue retroactively; customer notified   |

### 8.2 Determining Good Faith

A dispute is considered in good faith when:

- It references specific invoice line items
- It provides a contractual or factual basis for the objection
- Supporting documentation is included or reasonably available
- The customer is responsive to information requests

## 9. Escalation Procedures

### 9.1 Internal Escalation

| Trigger                                         | Escalation Level                  | Timeframe              |
| ----------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Dispute not resolved within 15 business days    | AR Manager to Director of Finance | Day 16                 |
| Customer rejects proposed resolution            | Director of Finance               | Within 2 business days |
| Disputed amount exceeds $25,000                 | CFO/Controller                    | Immediate              |
| Dispute involves potential legal liability      | Legal Department                  | Immediate              |
| Pattern of repeated disputes from same customer | VP of Sales + Director of Finance | Upon identification    |

### 9.2 Customer Escalation Path

If the customer is dissatisfied with the resolution, they may escalate as follows:

1. **Level 1:** AR Manager — [Name, phone, email]
2. **Level 2:** Director of Finance — [Name, phone, email]
3. **Level 3:** CFO/Controller — [Name, phone, email]
4. **Level 4:** Formal mediation or arbitration per contract terms

## 10. Reporting and Metrics

### 10.1 Key Performance Indicators

| Metric                                  | Target             | Frequency |
| --------------------------------------- | ------------------ | --------- |
| Average dispute resolution time         | ≤ 15 business days | Monthly   |
| Disputes resolved within SLA            | ≥ 90%              | Monthly   |
| Total disputed amount as % of revenue   | ≤ 2%               | Monthly   |
| Dispute recurrence rate (same customer) | ≤ 10%              | Quarterly |
| Customer satisfaction with resolution   | ≥ 4.0/5.0          | Quarterly |
| Credit memos as % of gross revenue      | ≤ 1.5%             | Monthly   |

### 10.2 Monthly Dispute Report

The AR Manager prepares a monthly report including:

- Number of disputes opened, resolved, and pending
- Total dollar amount in dispute
- Aging of open disputes
- Resolution type breakdown
- Root cause analysis of top dispute categories
- Recommendations for process improvements
