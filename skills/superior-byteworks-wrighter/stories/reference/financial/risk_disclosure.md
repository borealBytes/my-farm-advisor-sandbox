### Liquidity Risk

> [!NOTE]
> Liquidity risk refers to the possibility that you may not be able to sell your investment quickly at a fair price.

The Fund may not be able to sell securities quickly at prices approximating those at which they could currently be sold. This risk may be heightened during periods of market volatility or stress.

### Credit Risk

Credit risk is the risk that the issuer of a security will fail to make timely payments of principal and interest. If an issuer defaults, you may lose part or all of your investment.

## Specific Product Risks

### Derivatives Risk

> [!TIP]
> Derivatives can amplify both gains and losses. Only investors who understand these instruments should consider investments that use derivatives.

The Fund may use derivatives, including futures, options, and swaps. These instruments can be volatile and may involve leverage, which can magnify both gains and losses.

### Counterparty Risk

The Fund is subject to counterparty risk to the extent that it enters into derivatives transactions or securities lending arrangements. If a counterparty fails to meet its obligations, the Fund may experience losses.
