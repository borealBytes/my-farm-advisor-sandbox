## Pricing Philosophy

The pricing strategy balances three objectives:

1. **Margin protection** — Sustain gross margin targets that fund operations and growth.
2. **Competitive positioning** — Price within the acceptable range for the target customer segment.
3. **Perceived value** — Reinforce brand positioning through price-quality alignment.

> **Guiding principle**: We compete on value, not on lowest price. Price reductions are a planned tool, not a reactive habit.

## Markup & Margin Calculations

### Core Formulas

| Metric                              | Formula                                               | Example                               |
| ----------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| **Markup %**                        | (Retail Price − Cost) ÷ Cost × 100                    | ($50 − $20) ÷ $20 × 100 = **150%**    |
| **Gross Margin %**                  | (Retail Price − Cost) ÷ Retail Price × 100            | ($50 − $20) ÷ $50 × 100 = **60%**     |
| **Retail Price from target margin** | Cost ÷ (1 − Target Margin %)                          | $20 ÷ (1 − 0.60) = **$50.00**         |
| **Break-even units**                | Fixed Costs ÷ (Retail Price − Variable Cost per Unit) | $10,000 ÷ ($50 − $22) = **358 units** |

## Competitive Pricing Framework

### Price Positioning Tiers

| Tier              | Position vs. Market   | Applicable Categories                     | Rationale                                 |
| ----------------- | --------------------- | ----------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| **Price Leader**  | 5–10% below market    | KVIs (Known Value Items), traffic drivers | Draw foot traffic; build price perception |
| **Market Parity** | ±2% of market average | Core assortment, commodity items          | Match competition; avoid price wars       |
| **Premium**       | 5–15% above market    | Exclusive brands, own-label, experiential | Margin accretive; reinforces brand value  |

### Competitive Price Audits

| Element      | Standard                                                      |
| ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Frequency    | Monthly for A-class SKUs; quarterly for B/C                   |
| Sources      | Top 3 direct competitors (brick-and-mortar and online)        |
| Methodology  | Mystery shop + web scraping (automated where permitted)       |
| Output       | Competitive Price Index (CPI) report to Merchandising team    |
| Response SLA | Price adjustments on KVIs within 72 hours of audit completion |

### Competitive Price Index (CPI)

```
CPI = (Our Price ÷ Competitor Average Price) × 100

CPI = 100  → Parity
CPI > 100  → We are priced above market
CPI < 100  → We are priced below market
```

**Target CPI range**: 97–103 for core assortment.

## Price Change Procedures

### Price Change Types

| Type                                | Definition                                 | Approval Required           |
| ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | --------------------------- |
| **Initial price set**               | First retail price on a new SKU            | Buyer / Merchandise Planner |
| **Permanent price change**          | Ongoing retail adjustment (up or down)     | Buyer + VP Merchandising    |
| **Temporary price reduction (TPR)** | Time-limited promotional price             | Buyer + Promotions Manager  |
| **Markdown**                        | Permanent reduction to clear inventory     | Buyer + Inventory Planner   |
| **Clearance**                       | Final price to liquidate end-of-life stock | Buyer + VP Merchandising    |

### Price Change Workflow

| Step | Action                                              | Owner               | SLA               |
| ---- | --------------------------------------------------- | ------------------- | ----------------- |
| 1    | Submit price change request in Pricing System       | Buyer               | —                 |
| 2    | Margin impact analysis generated automatically      | System              | Instant           |
| 3    | Approver reviews margin impact and competitive data | Approver (per type) | 24 hours          |
| 4    | Approved changes batch-loaded to POS overnight      | Pricing Ops         | Next business day |
| 5    | Store validates shelf tags match POS price          | Store Team          | By store open     |
| 6    | Post-change sales velocity monitored for 7 days     | Merchandise Planner | Ongoing           |

## Markdown Strategy

### Markdown Cadence

| Week                | Markdown Depth | Trigger                            |
| ------------------- | -------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Week 8 of season    | 20% off        | Sell-through < 40%                 |
| Week 10             | 30% off        | Sell-through < 55%                 |
| Week 12             | 40% off        | Sell-through < 70%                 |
| Week 14 (clearance) | 50–70% off     | End-of-season; liquidate remaining |

### Markdown Rules

1. Markdowns apply only to **current season** merchandise unless authorized for carryover.
2. No markdown may reduce margin below **landed cost** without VP Merchandising approval.
3. Below-cost markdowns require **CFO sign-off** and must be tagged as clearance in the IMS.
4. Markdown budgets are set at the department level during Open-to-Buy planning.
5. Unplanned markdowns (damage, competitive reaction) charged to the requesting department.

### Sell-Through Formula

```
Sell-Through % = Units Sold ÷ (Units Sold + Units On-Hand) × 100
```

## Promotional Pricing

### Promotion Types

| Promo Type             | Example                     | Margin Impact                                                  | Best For                |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| **% Off**              | 20% off all denim           | Direct margin reduction                                        | Category-wide clearance |
| **$ Off**              | $10 off purchase of $50+    | Controlled discount; encourages basket build                   | Driving ATV             |
| **BOGO**               | Buy 1 Get 1 50% off         | Blended margin on pair; drives UPT                             | Volume categories       |
| **Gift with Purchase** | Free tote with $75 purchase | Cost of gift absorbed; margin on qualifying purchase preserved | Brand-building          |
| **Bundle**             | Shirt + Tie for $59.99      | Higher combined margin than individual markdown                | Coordinated categories  |
| **Loyalty Exclusive**  | Extra 10% for members       | Loyalty acquisition cost; funded by LTV increase               | Retention, data capture |

## Price Compliance

### Regulatory Requirements

| Requirement             | Standard                                                                                                     |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Price accuracy**      | Scanned price must match lowest displayed/advertised price                                                   |
| **Unit pricing**        | Required for consumables where mandated by state/provincial law                                              |
| **Comparative pricing** | "Was/Now" claims must reference a price at which item was offered for ≥ 28 consecutive days in prior 90 days |
| **Rain checks**         | Offered for advertised items that are out of stock, valid for 30 days                                        |
| **MAP compliance**      | Minimum Advertised Price enforced for brands with MAP agreements; violations reported to vendor relations    |

### Price Audit Procedure

| Step | Action                                            | Frequency |
| ---- | ------------------------------------------------- | --------- |
| 1    | Select 50 random SKUs across departments          | Weekly    |
| 2    | Scan at POS and compare to shelf tag / signage    | Weekly    |
| 3    | Record discrepancies in **Price Accuracy Log**    | As found  |
| 4    | Correct discrepancies within 2 hours of discovery | Immediate |
| 5    | Report accuracy rate to District Manager          | Weekly    |

**Target price accuracy rate**: ≥ 98%.

| ----------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ | ------------ |

| Competitive Price Index                   | 97–103                               | Monthly      |
| Price accuracy rate                       | ≥ 98%                                | Weekly audit |
| Gross margin return on investment (GMROI) | ≥ 2.0                                | Quarterly    |
| Sell-through at full price                | ≥ 60%                                | Seasonal     |

### GMROI Formula

```
GMROI = Gross Margin $ ÷ Average Inventory Cost

GMROI of 2.0 means $2.00 of gross margin for every $1.00 invested in inventory.
```
