## Article I — Defined Terms

**"Aggregate Data"** means data that has been combined and de-identified such that it cannot reasonably be linked to any individual.

**"Cookies"** means small text files placed on your device by the Service to collect standard internet log information and visitor behavior data.

**"Data Controller"** means the entity that determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Data, as defined under GDPR.

**"Data Processor"** means any entity that processes Personal Data on behalf of the Data Controller.

**"Data Subject"** means an identified or identifiable natural person whose Personal Data is processed.

**"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person, including but not limited to name, email address, IP address, device identifiers, and location data.

**"Processing"** means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, storage, use, transfer, and deletion.

**"Sensitive Personal Data"** means Personal Data revealing racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, health data, biometric data, or sexual orientation.

**"Third-Party Services"** means external services, platforms, or tools integrated with or accessible through the Service.

## Article II — Information We Collect

### Section 2.1 — Information You Provide Directly

We collect Personal Data that you voluntarily provide when using the Service, including:

- (a) **Account Information**: name, email address, username, password, and profile details;
- (b) **Billing Information**: payment card details, billing address, and transaction history;
- (c) **Communication Data**: messages, support requests, and feedback submitted to us;
- (d) **User Content**: files, data, text, and other materials you upload or submit;
- (e) **Survey and Research Data**: responses to surveys, questionnaires, or research participation.

### Section 2.2 — Information Collected Automatically

When you access the Service, we automatically collect certain information, including:

- (a) **Device Information**: device type, operating system, browser type, and screen resolution;
- (b) **Log Data**: IP address, access times, pages viewed, referring URL, and clickstream data;
- (c) **Usage Data**: feature usage patterns, session duration, and interaction metrics;
- (d) **Location Data**: approximate geographic location derived from IP address;
- (e) **Cookie Data**: information collected through Cookies and similar tracking technologies.

### Section 2.3 — Information from Third Parties

We may receive Personal Data from third-party sources, including:

- (a) **Single Sign-On Providers**: authentication data from Google, Microsoft, or other identity providers;
- (b) **Analytics Providers**: aggregated usage and demographic data;
- (c) **Business Partners**: information shared pursuant to joint marketing or integration agreements;
- (d) **Public Sources**: publicly available information from social media profiles or public databases.

### Section 8.1 — Technical Measures

We implement industry-standard technical safeguards, including: (a) encryption of data in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256); (b) access controls and multi-factor authentication; (c) regular vulnerability assessments and penetration testing; (d) intrusion detection and monitoring systems.

### Section 8.2 — Organizational Measures

We maintain organizational safeguards, including: (a) employee training on data protection and security; (b) access restricted on a need-to-know basis; (c) incident response procedures; (d) vendor due diligence and Data Processing Agreements.

### Section 8.3 — Breach Notification

In the event of a Personal Data breach, we will: (a) notify the relevant supervisory authority within seventy-two (72) hours of becoming aware of the breach, where required by law; (b) notify affected Data Subjects without undue delay when the breach poses a high risk to their rights and freedoms; and (c) document all breaches and remediation actions.

## Article IX — Your Rights

### Section 9.1 — Rights Under GDPR

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), you have the following rights:

- (a) **Right of Access** — request a copy of your Personal Data;
- (b) **Right to Rectification** — request correction of inaccurate data;
- (c) **Right to Erasure** — request deletion of your Personal Data ("right to be forgotten");
- (d) **Right to Restriction** — request limitation of Processing;
- (e) **Right to Portability** — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format;
- (f) **Right to Object** — object to Processing based on legitimate interests;
- (g) **Right to Withdraw Consent** — withdraw consent at any time without affecting prior Processing.

### Section 9.2 — Rights Under CCPA

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights:

- (a) **Right to Know** — request disclosure of Personal Data collected, used, and shared;
- (b) **Right to Delete** — request deletion of Personal Data;
- (c) **Right to Opt-Out** — opt out of the sale of Personal Data (note: we do not sell data);
- (d) **Right to Non-Discrimination** — exercise rights without discriminatory treatment.

### Section 9.3 — Exercising Your Rights

To exercise any of the above rights, contact our Data Protection Officer at [DPO EMAIL]. We will respond within thirty (30) days (GDPR) or forty-five (45) days (CCPA) of receiving a verified request.
