## One-Pager Writing Rules

> _Remove this section before distributing_

**The Iron Constraints:**

- **One page.** If it doesn't fit, cut — don't shrink the font below 10pt.
- **One ask.** Multiple asks = zero asks. Pick the most important.
- **One audience.** Write for one specific person, not "stakeholders."

**Structure (AIDA):**

1. **Attention** — Hook with the problem or a striking fact
2. **Interest** — Explain the solution and why it's different
3. **Desire** — Show proof (results, traction, social proof)
4. **Action** — One clear, low-friction next step

**Editing Checklist:**

- [ ] Can a stranger understand this in 60 seconds?
- [ ] Is every sentence earning its place? (Delete anything that doesn't advance the argument)
- [ ] Is the ask crystal clear?
- [ ] Are all numbers specific? (Not "significant growth" → "47% YoY growth")
- [ ] Is the contact information visible?
- [ ] Does it fit on one page at 10pt font with 0.75" margins?

**Common Mistakes:**

- ❌ Too much background — get to the point in sentence 1
- ❌ Feature list instead of benefit statements
- ❌ Weak CTA ("feel free to reach out") → Strong CTA ("Book a 20-min call: [link]")
- ❌ No social proof — add a number, a customer name, or a quote
- ❌ Passive voice — use active, energetic language
