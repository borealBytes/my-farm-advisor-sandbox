## Deck Structure Overview

> **The Sequoia Framework:** Problem → Solution → Why Now → Market → Product → Business Model → Traction → Team → Financials → Ask
>
> **YC Principle:** "Make something people want." Every slide should answer: "Why does this matter?"
>
> **Airbnb Rule:** Tell a story. Investors fund people and narratives, not spreadsheets.

## Pitch Deck Best Practices

> _Remove this section before distributing_

**Format:**

- **Slides:** 10–15 slides for the main deck (Sequoia recommends 10)
- **Font:** 24pt+ for body text — readable from the back of a room
- **Colors:** 2–3 colors maximum; high contrast
- **Images:** High quality; no stock photos of handshakes
- **Data:** One key insight per chart; label axes; cite sources

**Storytelling:**

- Open with the problem, not your company history
- Use the "hero's journey": customer has a problem → discovers your solution → transforms
- Every slide should answer: "Why does this matter to the investor?"

**Delivery:**

- **Time:** 10–15 minutes for the deck; 30–45 minutes total with Q&A
- **Practice:** Know every number cold — investors will probe
- **Questions:** The best sign is lots of questions — it means they're engaged
- **Follow-up:** Send the deck within 24 hours of the meeting

**Common Mistakes:**

- ❌ Too many slides (>20 for main deck)
- ❌ Too much text — if you're reading slides, you've lost them
- ❌ Unrealistic projections without assumptions
- ❌ Dismissing competition ("we have no competitors")
- ❌ No clear ask — what do you want from this meeting?
- ❌ Founder bios that list titles instead of accomplishments
- ❌ TAM that's too small (<$1B) or too large (entire global economy)
