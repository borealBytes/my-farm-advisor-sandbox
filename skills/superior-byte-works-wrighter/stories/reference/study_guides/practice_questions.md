## Section 1: [Section Name]

### Question 1.1 _(Easy)_

**Topic:** [Topic A — e.g., Economic Indicators]

[Question text. E.g., "Which of the following is considered a leading economic indicator?"]

- A) [Unemployment rate]
- B) [Consumer Price Index]
- C) [Building permits]
- D) [Average duration of unemployment]

<details>
<summary>Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** [Building permits are a leading indicator because they predict future construction activity. The unemployment rate (A) and average duration of unemployment (D) are lagging indicators — they confirm trends already underway. CPI (B) is a coincident/lagging indicator.]

**Key Rule:** [Leading indicators predict economic activity; lagging indicators confirm it; coincident indicators move with the economy.]

**Reference:** [Section Guide — Topic A: Economic Indicators] | [Textbook Ch. X, p. XX]

**Common Mistake:** [Confusing unemployment rate (lagging) with initial jobless claims (leading).]

</details>

### Question 1.2 _(Medium)_

**Topic:** [Topic B — e.g., Financial Ratios]

[Question text. E.g., "A company reports the following: Current Assets $800,000; Inventory $200,000; Current Liabilities $400,000. What is the quick ratio?"]

- A) [1.0]
- B) [1.5]
- C) [2.0]
- D) [3.0]

<details>
<summary>Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:**

$$\text{Quick Ratio} = \frac{\text{Current Assets} - \text{Inventory}}{\text{Current Liabilities}} = \frac{\$800{,}000 - \$200{,}000}{\$400{,}000} = \frac{\$600{,}000}{\$400{,}000} = 1.5$$

[The quick ratio excludes inventory from current assets because inventory is the least liquid current asset. A quick ratio of 1.5 means the company has $1.50 in liquid assets for every $1 in current liabilities.]

**Key Rule:** [Quick Ratio = (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities. Also called the "acid-test ratio."]

**Reference:** [Section Guide — Topic B: Financial Statements] | [Textbook Ch. X, p. XX]

**Common Mistake:** [Using total assets instead of current assets, or forgetting to subtract inventory.]

</details>

### Question 1.3 _(Hard)_

**Topic:** [Topic C — e.g., Quantitative Methods]

[Question text. E.g., "An investor purchases a bond at par ($1,000) with a 5% coupon, paid semi-annually. If the YTM rises to 6%, which of the following is TRUE?"]

- A) [The bond price will increase above $1,000]
- B) [The bond price will decrease below $1,000]
- C) [The bond price will remain at $1,000]
- D) [The coupon payment will increase to 6%]

<details>
<summary>Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** [When market interest rates (YTM) rise above the coupon rate, the bond's price falls below par to compensate buyers for the below-market coupon. The coupon rate is fixed at issuance and does not change (eliminating D). Bond prices and yields move inversely.]

**Key Rule:** [Bond prices and interest rates have an INVERSE relationship. When rates rise, prices fall; when rates fall, prices rise.]

**Reference:** [Section Guide — Topic C: Debt Securities] | [Textbook Ch. X, p. XX]

**Common Mistake:** [Choosing A — thinking higher YTM means higher value. Remember: higher yield required = lower price paid.]

</details>

### Question 3.2 _(Medium)_

**Topic:** [Topic B — e.g., Client Suitability]

[Scenario-based question. E.g., "A 67-year-old retiree with moderate risk tolerance, a $1.2M portfolio, and $50,000 annual income needs asks her adviser for a recommendation. Which is MOST suitable?"]

- A) [100% equity growth portfolio]
- B) [60/40 balanced portfolio with income focus]
- C) [Concentrated position in employer stock]
- D) [Leveraged ETF portfolio]

<details>
<summary>Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** [A balanced 60/40 portfolio with income focus matches the client's moderate risk tolerance, retirement status (income needs), and time horizon. 100% equity (A) is too aggressive for a retiree. Concentrated employer stock (C) lacks diversification. Leveraged ETFs (D) are unsuitable for retirement income portfolios.]

**Key Rule:** [Suitability requires matching recommendations to client's age, risk tolerance, time horizon, liquidity needs, and investment objectives.]

**Reference:** [Section Guide — Topic B: Suitability] | [Textbook Ch. X, p. XX]

**Common Mistake:** [Focusing only on risk tolerance while ignoring time horizon and income needs.]
