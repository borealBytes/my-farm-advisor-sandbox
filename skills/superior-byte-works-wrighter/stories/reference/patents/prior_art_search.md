### 4.2 Non-Obviousness Analysis (35 U.S.C. § 103)

**Standard:** A claim is obvious if the differences between the invention and the prior art are such that the subject matter as a whole would have been obvious to a person having ordinary skill in the art (PHOSITA) at the time of invention. See _KSR Int'l Co. v. Teleflex Inc._, 550 U.S. 398 (2007).
