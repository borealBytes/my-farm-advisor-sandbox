## Subject-Specific Rubric Examples

### Writing Rubric

| Criterion         | Exemplary                                                  | Proficient                                   | Developing                               | Beginning                            |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| **Ideas/Content** | Clear, focused, original ideas with insightful development | Clear main idea with adequate support        | Main idea present but development uneven | Unclear or underdeveloped ideas      |
| **Organization**  | Logical, effective structure with smooth transitions       | Clear structure with appropriate transitions | Structure apparent but inconsistent      | Lacks clear organization             |
| **Voice/Tone**    | Strong, consistent voice appropriate to audience           | Appropriate voice for purpose                | Voice inconsistent or inappropriate      | Voice unclear or inappropriate       |
| **Word Choice**   | Precise, vivid, varied vocabulary                          | Clear, appropriate word choice               | Generic or repetitive word choice        | Limited or inappropriate vocabulary  |
| **Conventions**   | Few or no errors; enhances readability                     | Minor errors that don't impede understanding | Errors distract from meaning             | Frequent errors impede understanding |

## Using This Rubric

### For Students

> [!TIP]

- Share rubrics with students before they begin work. Rubrics clarify expectations and support self-assessment.

**Before Assignment:**

- Review rubric with students
- Discuss exemplars at each level
- Answer questions about criteria
- Allow students to ask for clarification

**During Assignment:**

- Reference rubric criteria
- Encourage self-assessment
- Provide formative feedback

**After Assignment:**

- Return rubric with feedback
- Allow students to reflect
- Offer opportunities for revision if applicable

### For Teachers

**Scoring Tips:**

- Use rubric consistently across all students
- Score "blind" when possible (without looking at student names)
- Calibrate with other teachers if team grading
- Document evidence for scores given

**Feedback Guidelines:**

- Reference specific criteria
- Provide actionable suggestions
- Balance strengths and areas for growth
- Connect to learning objectives

### Rubric Validation

**Checklist:**

- [ ] Criteria align with learning objectives
- [ ] Performance levels are distinct
- [ ] Descriptors are specific and observable
- [ ] Examples are provided for clarity
- [ ] Weighting reflects importance
- [ ] Students can understand expectations
- [ ] Scoring is feasible and reliable
