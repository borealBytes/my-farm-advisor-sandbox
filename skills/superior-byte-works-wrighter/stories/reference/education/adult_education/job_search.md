## Resume Writing

### Resume Formats

**Chronological (Most Common)**
- Best for: Steady work history
- Lists jobs in reverse chronological order
- Emphasizes career progression

**Functional**
- Best for: Career changers, employment gaps
- Focuses on skills over job history
- Groups experience by skill areas

**Combination**
- Best for: Most situations
- Skills section + chronological work history
- Balances both approaches

### Resume Writing Tips

**DO:**
- ✅ Use action verbs (Led, Managed, Created, Improved)
- ✅ Quantify achievements ("Increased sales by 25%")
- ✅ Tailor to each job (match keywords)
- ✅ Keep to 1-2 pages
- ✅ Use professional format
- ✅ Proofread carefully

**DON'T:**
- ❌ Include personal information (age, marital status, photo)
- ❌ Use "I" statements
- ❌ Include irrelevant jobs from 15+ years ago
- ❌ Use generic objectives
- ❌ Have typos or errors

### Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS)

**How ATS Works:**
1. Parses resume for keywords
2. Scores based on match
3. Top scores go to human recruiters

**Beat the ATS:**
- Use standard section headings
- Include keywords from job description
- Avoid tables, graphics, headers/footers
- Save as .docx or plain .pdf
- Spell out acronyms first

**ATS-Friendly Template:**
```
PROFESSIONAL EXPERIENCE

Software Engineer | ABC Company | New York, NY
January 2020 - Present

- Developed web applications using JavaScript, React, and Node.js
- Improved application performance by 40% through code optimization
- Collaborated with cross-functional teams using Agile methodologies
```

## Cover Letters

### Cover Letter Structure

**Header:**
```
Your Name
Your Address
Your Phone | Your Email

Date

Hiring Manager Name (if known)
Company Name
Company Address
```

**Opening (Paragraph 1):**
- Hook their attention
- State position you're applying for
- Mention how you found the job

**Example:**
"I am writing to express my strong interest in the Marketing Manager position at XYZ Company, which I discovered through LinkedIn. With my 7 years of experience driving successful marketing campaigns in the tech industry, I am excited about the opportunity to contribute to your team."

**Body (Paragraphs 2-3):**
- Connect your experience to job requirements
- Provide specific examples
- Show knowledge of company

**Example:**
"In my current role at ABC Tech, I have led marketing initiatives that increased lead generation by 35% year-over-year. I specialize in digital marketing strategy, including SEO, content marketing, and social media campaigns. Recently, I spearheaded a product launch that exceeded revenue targets by $2M in its first quarter.

I am particularly drawn to XYZ Company because of your commitment to innovation and your recent expansion into sustainable technology. Your mission aligns with my passion for marketing products that make a positive impact."

**Closing (Paragraph 4):**
- Express enthusiasm
- Call to action
- Thank them

**Example:**
"I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my experience in strategic marketing can contribute to XYZ Company's continued growth. Thank you for considering my application. I look forward to hearing from you."

## Interview Preparation

### Types of Interviews

**Phone/Video Screen:**
- 15-30 minutes
- Basic qualifications
- Salary expectations
- Availability

**Behavioral Interview:**
- "Tell me about a time when..."
- Uses STAR method
- Past behavior predicts future

**Technical Interview:**
- Skills demonstration
- Problem-solving
- Case studies
- Work samples

**Panel Interview:**
- Multiple interviewers
- Various perspectives
- Group dynamics

**Final Interview:**
- Senior leadership
- Cultural fit
- Final questions

### The STAR Method

**S - Situation:** Set the scene
**T - Task:** Explain your responsibility
**A - Action:** Describe what you did
**R - Result:** Share the outcome (quantify!)

**Example:**
"Tell me about a time you dealt with a difficult customer."

**Situation:**
"At my previous job in customer service, I received a call from a customer who was extremely frustrated because their order had been delayed twice and they needed the product for an important presentation."

**Task:**
"My task was to resolve the issue quickly while maintaining the customer relationship and ensuring they received their order in time."

**Action:**
"I immediately apologized for the inconvenience, then contacted our warehouse to identify the fastest shipping option. I discovered we could expedite the order with overnight delivery at no extra cost. I also arranged for a 20% discount on their next purchase as compensation for the delays. I then called the customer back within 30 minutes with this solution."

**Result:**
"The customer received their order the next morning in time for their presentation. They sent a thank-you email to my manager, and they became one of our most loyal customers, placing three more orders that quarter totaling over $15,000."

### Common Interview Questions

**Tell me about yourself.**
"I have {{YEARS}} years of experience in {{FIELD}}, specializing in {{SPECIALTY}}. In my current role at {{COMPANY}}, I've {{KEY_ACHIEVEMENT_1}} and {{KEY_ACHIEVEMENT_2}}. I'm particularly passionate about {{PASSION}}, which is why I'm excited about this opportunity at {{TARGET_COMPANY}}."

**Why do you want to work here?**
"I'm drawn to {{COMPANY}} because of {{SPECIFIC_REASON_1}} and {{SPECIFIC_REASON_2}}. I admire {{SPECIFIC_PROJECT_OR_VALUE}} and believe my experience in {{YOUR_EXPERTISE}} would allow me to contribute meaningfully to your team."

**What are your greatest strengths?**
"One of my key strengths is {{STRENGTH_1}}. For example, {{EXAMPLE_1}}. Another strength is {{STRENGTH_2}}, which I've demonstrated by {{EXAMPLE_2}}."

**What is your greatest weakness?**
"I've been working on {{WEAKNESS}}. I recognized this when {{SITUATION}}, so I {{ACTION_TAKEN}}. As a result, {{IMPROVEMENT}}."

**Why are you leaving your current job?**
"I'm looking for {{WHAT_YOU_SEEK}}. While I've learned a lot at {{CURRENT_COMPANY}}, I believe {{TARGET_COMPANY}} offers {{OPPORTUNITY}}."

**Where do you see yourself in 5 years?**
"My goal is to {{CAREER_GOAL}}. I see this role as {{HOW_THIS_ROLE_FITS}}. I'm committed to {{COMMITMENT}}."

**What are your salary expectations?**
"Based on my research of similar positions in {{LOCATION}} and considering my {{YEARS}} years of experience, I'm looking for {{SALARY_RANGE}}. However, I'm open to discussion based on the total compensation package."

**Do you have any questions for us?**
- What does success look like in this role in the first 90 days?
- What are the biggest challenges facing the team right now?
- How would you describe the company culture?
- What do you enjoy most about working here?
- What are the next steps in the hiring process?

### Negotiation Strategies

**When They Make an Offer:**
1. Express enthusiasm
2. Ask for time to consider (24-48 hours)
3. Evaluate the entire package
4. Prepare counteroffer
5. Negotiate respectfully

**Total Compensation Package:**
- Base salary
- Bonus/commission
- Benefits (health, dental, vision)
- Retirement (401k match)
- Stock options/equity
- PTO/vacation
- Professional development
- Flexible work arrangements
- Equipment stipend
- Commuter benefits

**Negotiation Script:**

"Thank you for this offer. I'm very excited about the opportunity to join {{COMPANY}} and contribute to {{TEAM/PROJECT}}.

Based on my research and considering my {{YEARS}} years of experience in {{FIELD}}, I was expecting a salary in the range of {{TARGET_RANGE}}. Would there be flexibility to move closer to that figure?"

**Alternative: Benefits Negotiation**

"I understand there may be constraints on the base salary. Would there be flexibility in other areas of the compensation package, such as:
- Additional PTO days?
- Professional development budget?
- Flexible work schedule?
- Remote work options?
- Signing bonus?"

**What NOT to Do:**
- Don't accept immediately without consideration
- Don't give specific number first (if possible)
- Don't negotiate until you have written offer
- Don't make demands, make requests
- Don't forget non-salary benefits
