# Basic Math Skills for Adults

## Course Overview

This course covers essential mathematics needed for daily life, employment, and further education. No prior math knowledge required.

**Duration:** 12 weeks (3 hours/week)
**Prerequisites:** None
**Materials:** Calculator (basic or TI-30XS), notebook, pencils
**Goals:** By the end, you'll confidently handle: whole numbers, fractions, decimals, percents, basic algebra, geometry, and data analysis.

## Module 1: Whole Numbers & Operations (Weeks 1-2)

### Learning Objectives
By the end of this module, you will:
- Add, subtract, multiply, and divide whole numbers
- Understand place value (ones, tens, hundreds, thousands)
- Solve word problems using all four operations
- Check your work using estimation

### Lesson 1.1: Addition & Subtraction

**Key Concepts:**
- Place value: In 4,573, the 5 represents 500
- Carrying (addition): When a column sums to 10+, carry to next column
- Borrowing (subtraction): When top digit is smaller, borrow from next column

**Step-by-Step Examples:**

**Addition Example:** 456 + 378
```
  456
+ 378
-----
  834

Step 1: 6 + 8 = 14 (write 4, carry 1)
Step 2: 5 + 7 + 1 (carried) = 13 (write 3, carry 1)
Step 3: 4 + 3 + 1 (carried) = 8 (write 8)
```

**Subtraction Example:** 623 - 487
```
  623
- 487
-----
  136

Step 1: 3 - 7 (can't do, borrow from 2)
        13 - 7 = 6
Step 2: 1 (after borrowing) - 8 (can't do, borrow from 6)
        11 - 8 = 3
Step 3: 5 (after borrowing) - 4 = 1
```

**Practice Problems with Solutions:**

**Set A: Addition (Check with calculator)**
1. 245 + 367 = ? 
   *Answer: 612*
2. 1,489 + 2,543 = ?
   *Answer: 4,032*
3. 56 + 89 + 34 = ?
   *Answer: 179*

**Set B: Subtraction**

   *Answer: 266*

   *Answer: 544*
3. 8,432 - 5,678 = ?
   *Answer: 2,754*

**Word Problems:**
1. Maria earns $485 per week. Her bills total $367. How much does she have left?
   *Solution: $485 - $367 = $118 left*

2. A store sold 234 items on Monday and 189 on Tuesday. Total sold?
   *Solution: 234 + 189 = 423 items*

### Lesson 1.2: Multiplication & Division

**Key Concepts:**
- Multiplication: Repeated addition (3 × 4 = 3 + 3 + 3 + 3 = 12)
- Times tables: Memorize 1-12 (use flashcards daily)
- Division: Inverse of multiplication (12 ÷ 4 = 3)
- Long division: Divide, Multiply, Subtract, Bring down, Repeat

**Step-by-Step Examples:**

**Multiplication Example:** 24 × 36
```
    24
   ×36
   ----
   144    (24 × 6)
  +720    (24 × 30, shifted left)
   ----
   864
```

**Long Division Example:** 864 ÷ 24
```
     36
   -----
24 ) 864
     72    (24 × 3 = 72)
     ---
     144
     144   (24 × 6 = 144)
     ---
       0
```

**Practice Problems:**

**Set C: Multiplication**

2. 23 × 45 = ? (*Answer: 1,035*)

**Set D: Division**

2. 2,835 ÷ 45 = ? (*Answer: 63*)
3. 1,000 ÷ 25 = ? (*Answer: 40*)

**Real-World Applications:**
1. If apples cost $3 per pound and you buy 8 pounds, total cost?
   *Solution: $3 × 8 = $24*

2. If you have $180 to divide equally among 6 people?
   *Solution: $180 ÷ 6 = $30 each*

### Lesson 1.3: Order of Operations (PEMDAS)

**PEMDAS:**
- **P**arentheses first
- **E**xponents (powers)
- **M**ultiplication & **D**ivision (left to right)
- **A**ddition & **S**ubtraction (left to right)

**Memory Aid:** "Please Excuse My Dear Aunt Sally"

**Examples:**

1. 3 + 4 × 5
   - Multiply first: 4 × 5 = 20
   - Then add: 3 + 20 = **23**
   - *Wrong: 3 + 4 = 7, then 7 × 5 = 35 (WRONG!)*

2. (12 - 4) ÷ 2 + 3
   - Parentheses: 12 - 4 = 8
   - Division: 8 ÷ 2 = 4
   - Addition: 4 + 3 = **7**

3. 20 ÷ (4 + 1) × 2
   - Parentheses: 4 + 1 = 5
   - Division: 20 ÷ 5 = 4
   - Multiplication: 4 × 2 = **8**

**Practice:**
1. 10 - 3 + 2 = ? (*Answer: 9*)
2. 18 ÷ 3 + 7 = ? (*Answer: 13*)
3. (15 - 5) × 3 = ? (*Answer: 30*)
4. 8 + 4 × 2 - 6 = ? (*Answer: 10*)

## Module 2: Fractions (Weeks 3-4)

### Learning Objectives
- Understand what fractions represent
- Simplify fractions
- Add, subtract, multiply, and divide fractions
- Convert between mixed numbers and improper fractions

### Lesson 2.1: Understanding Fractions

**Key Concepts:**
- Numerator (top): Parts you have
- Denominator (bottom): Total parts
- Example: 3/4 means 3 parts out of 4 total

**Visual Representation:**
```
[■■■| ]  3/4 = 3 shaded parts out of 4 total
[■■■■]
```

**Types of Fractions:**
- **Proper:** Numerator < denominator (3/4)
- **Improper:** Numerator ≥ denominator (7/4)
- **Mixed Number:** Whole number + fraction (1¾)

**Converting Mixed to Improper:**
1¾ = (1 × 4 + 3)/4 = 7/4

**Converting Improper to Mixed:**
7/4 = 1 remainder 3 = 1¾

**Simplifying Fractions:**
Divide numerator and denominator by same number
- 4/8 = ½ (divide by 4)
- 15/25 = 3/5 (divide by 5)
- 12/16 = ¾ (divide by 4)

**Finding Common Denominators:**
For ½ and ¼, common denominator is 4
For ⅓ and ¼, common denominator is 12

### Lesson 2.2: Adding & Subtracting Fractions

**Rule:** Must have same denominator

**Adding with Same Denominator:**
¼ + ¼ = 2/4 = ½

**Adding with Different Denominators:**
Step 1: Find common denominator
Step 2: Convert both fractions
Step 3: Add numerators

**Example:** ½ + ¼
- Common denominator: 4
- Convert: ½ = 2/4, ¼ = ¼
- Add: 2/4 + 1/4 = 3/4

**Example with Larger Numbers:** ⅔ + ¾
- Common denominator: 12
- Convert: ⅔ = 8/12, ¾ = 9/12
- Add: 8/12 + 9/12 = 17/12 = 1 5/12

**Subtracting Fractions:**
Same process as adding

**Example:** ¾ - ½
- Common denominator: 4
- Convert: ¾ = ¾, ½ = 2/4
- Subtract: ¾ - 2/4 = ¼

**Practice:**
1. ⅓ + ⅓ = ? (*Answer: ⅔*)
2. ½ + ¼ = ? (*Answer: ¾*)
3. ¾ - ¼ = ? (*Answer: ½*)
4. ⅔ + ¾ = ? (*Answer: 1 5/12*)
5. 1⅔ - ¾ = ? (*Answer: 11/12*)

### Lesson 2.3: Multiplying & Dividing Fractions

**Multiplication (easier than adding!):**
- Multiply numerators
- Multiply denominators
- Simplify

**Example:** ½ × ¼ = 1/8

**Example:** ⅔ × ¾
- Numerators: 2 × 3 = 6
- Denominators: 3 × 4 = 12
- Result: 6/12 = ½

**Shortcut (Canceling):**
Before multiplying, simplify crosswise
⅔ × ¾ → 2 and 4 can both divide by 2
= ⅓ × ¾ = ½

**Division:**
Flip second fraction, then multiply
**Example:** ½ ÷ ¼
- Flip: ½ × 4/1
- Multiply: 4/2 = 2

**Example:** ⅔ ÷ ¾
- Flip: ⅔ × 4/3
- Multiply: 8/9

**Practice:**
1. ½ × ⅓ = ? (*Answer: ⅙*)
2. ¾ × ⅘ = ? (*Answer: 3/5*)
3. ½ ÷ ⅛ = ? (*Answer: 4*)
4. ⅔ ÷ ⅓ = ? (*Answer: 2*)

### Assessment: Module 2

**Quiz:**
1. Simplify: 8/12 = ______
2. ½ + ⅓ = ______
3. ¾ - ½ = ______
4. ⅔ × ¾ = ______
5. ½ ÷ ¼ = ______
6. Convert: 2¾ = ______ (improper fraction)
7. Convert: 15/4 = ______ (mixed number)
8. Word: Recipe needs ⅔ cup flour. Making ½ recipe. Amount needed?
   *Answer: ⅔ × ½ = ⅓ cup*

## Module 3: Decimals & Money (Weeks 5-6)

### Learning Objectives
- Understand decimal place value
- Add, subtract, multiply, and divide decimals
- Round decimals
- Work with money calculations

### Lesson 3.1: Decimal Place Value

**Understanding Decimals:**
```
4,573.268

4 = 4,000 (thousands)
5 = 500 (hundreds)
7 = 70 (tens)
3 = 3 (ones)
. (decimal point)
2 = 2/10 (tenths)
6 = 6/100 (hundredths)
8 = 8/1000 (thousandths)
```

**Reading Decimals:**
- 0.5 = "five tenths" or "point five"
- 0.25 = "twenty-five hundredths"
- 3.75 = "three and seventy-five hundredths"

**Fraction ↔ Decimal Conversions:**
- ½ = 0.5
- ¼ = 0.25
- ¾ = 0.75
- ⅕ = 0.2
- ⅛ = 0.125

### Lesson 3.2: Decimal Operations

**Adding/Subtracting:**
Line up decimal points

**Example:** 3.45 + 2.8
```
  3.45
+ 2.80
------
  6.25
```

**Multiplying:**
- Multiply as whole numbers
- Count total decimal places in factors
- Product has that many decimal places

**Example:** 2.5 × 1.5
- 25 × 15 = 375
- One decimal place in each factor = 2 total
- Answer: 3.75

**Dividing:**
- Move decimal in divisor to make whole number
- Move decimal in dividend same places
- Divide normally

**Example:** 4.5 ÷ 0.9
- Move: 45 ÷ 9 = 5
- Answer: 5

**Practice:**
1. 2.45 + 3.8 = ? (*Answer: 6.25*)
2. 5.6 - 2.34 = ? (*Answer: 3.26*)
3. 2.5 × 4 = ? (*Answer: 10*)
4. 1.2 × 0.3 = ? (*Answer: 0.36*)
5. 4.8 ÷ 1.2 = ? (*Answer: 4*)

### Lesson 3.3: Money Math

**Real-World Applications:**

**Calculating Discounts:**
20% off $80 item:
- Discount: $80 × 0.20 = $16
- Sale price: $80 - $16 = $64

**Adding Sales Tax:**
8% tax on $50:
- Tax: $50 × 0.08 = $4
- Total: $50 + $4 = $54

**Tips:**
15% tip on $40 bill:
- Tip: $40 × 0.15 = $6
- Total: $40 + $6 = $46

**Unit Prices:**
Comparing:
- 12 oz for $3.60 → $0.30/oz
- 20 oz for $5.00 → $0.25/oz
*The 20 oz is better value*

**Practice:**
1. Item costs $45. 15% off. Sale price?
2. Restaurant bill $32. 18% tip. Total?
3. Which is better: 24-pack for $6 or 12-pack for $2.50?

## Module 4: Percents (Week 7)

### Learning Objectives
- Convert between percents, decimals, and fractions
- Calculate percentages
- Solve percent word problems
- Apply to real-life situations (tax, tip, discount)

### Lesson 4.1: Understanding Percents

**Percent = "Per Hundred"**
- 50% = 50/100 = ½ = 0.5
- 25% = 25/100 = ¼ = 0.25
- 100% = 1 (whole thing)

**Conversions:**

**Percent → Decimal:**
Move decimal 2 places left
- 45% = 0.45
- 8% = 0.08
- 125% = 1.25

**Decimal → Percent:**
Move decimal 2 places right
- 0.75 = 75%
- 0.4 = 40%
- 1.5 = 150%

**Fraction → Percent:**
Divide, then convert to percent
- ¾ = 0.75 = 75%
- ⅖ = 0.4 = 40%

### Lesson 4.2: Calculating Percentages

**Finding % of a Number:**
Convert % to decimal, then multiply

**Examples:**
- 20% of 150 = 0.20 × 150 = 30
- 15% of $80 = 0.15 × 80 = $12
- 8% tax on $50 = 0.08 × 50 = $4

**Finding What % One Number Is of Another:**
Divide, then convert to percent

**Examples:**
- 30 is what % of 150?
  30 ÷ 150 = 0.20 = 20%

- What % is $15 tip of $75 bill?
  15 ÷ 75 = 0.20 = 20%

**Finding Original Amount:**
Divide by percent (as decimal)

**Example:**
- If 20% of a number is 40, what's the number?
  40 ÷ 0.20 = 200

- If $20 is 25% off, original price?
  $20 is 75% of original
  $20 ÷ 0.75 = $26.67

### Lesson 4.3: Percent Applications

**Discount Problems:**
Original: $80, Discount: 25%
- Discount amount: $80 × 0.25 = $20
- Sale price: $80 - $20 = $60
- *Or: $80 × 0.75 = $60*

**Tax Problems:**
Price: $120, Tax: 8%
- Tax amount: $120 × 0.08 = $9.60
- Total: $120 + $9.60 = $129.60
- *Or: $120 × 1.08 = $129.60*

**Tip Problems:**
Bill: $45, Tip: 20%
- Tip: $45 × 0.20 = $9
- Total: $45 + $9 = $54

**Interest (Simple):**
Principal: $1,000, Rate: 5%, Time: 2 years
- Interest = $1,000 × 0.05 × 2 = $100
- Total = $1,000 + $100 = $1,100

**Practice:**
1. 15% of 200 = ______
2. 40 is what % of 200? ______
3. Original price $120. 30% off. Sale price?
4. Item costs $85. 7% tax. Total?
5. Bill is $60. 15% tip. Total?

## Module 5: Algebra Basics (Weeks 8-9)

### Learning Objectives
- Understand variables and expressions
- Solve one-step and two-step equations
- Work with inequalities
- Solve word problems using algebra

### Lesson 5.1: Variables & Expressions

**Variables:** Letters representing unknown numbers
- x, y, z, a, b, c

**Expressions:**
- 3x means 3 × x
- x + 5 means "x plus 5"
- 2x - 7 means "2 times x minus 7"

**Evaluating Expressions:**
If x = 4:
- 3x = 3 × 4 = 12
- x + 5 = 4 + 5 = 9
- 2x - 7 = 8 - 7 = 1

**Combining Like Terms:**
- 3x + 5x = 8x
- 7y - 2y = 5y
- 2x + 3y - x + y = x + 4y

### Lesson 5.2: Solving One-Step Equations

**Goal:** Get variable alone on one side

**Rule:** Do opposite operation to both sides

**Addition/Subtraction:**
- x + 5 = 12
  Subtract 5: x = 7

- x - 8 = 15
  Add 8: x = 23

**Multiplication/Division:**
- 3x = 18
  Divide by 3: x = 6

- x/4 = 5
  Multiply by 4: x = 20

**Examples:**
1. x + 7 = 20 → x = 13
2. x - 12 = 8 → x = 20
3. 5x = 35 → x = 7
4. x ÷ 6 = 4 → x = 24

### Lesson 5.3: Solving Two-Step Equations

**Process:**
1. Undo addition/subtraction
2. Undo multiplication/division

**Example:** 2x + 5 = 17
- Subtract 5: 2x = 12
- Divide by 2: x = 6

**Example:** 3x - 8 = 16
- Add 8: 3x = 24
- Divide by 3: x = 8

**Example with fraction:** ½x + 4 = 10
- Subtract 4: ½x = 6
- Multiply by 2: x = 12

**Word Problems to Equations:**

"Four more than twice a number is 18"
- Let x = the number
- 2x + 4 = 18
- 2x = 14
- x = 7

"Maria has $45, which is $12 more than twice what John has"
- Let j = John's amount
- 2j + 12 = 45
- 2j = 33
- j = $16.50

**Practice:**
1. 3x + 7 = 22 → x = ?
2. 5x - 8 = 27 → x = ?
3. x/3 + 4 = 9 → x = ?
4. Twice a number plus 5 is 31. What's the number?

## Module 6: Geometry Basics (Week 10)

### Learning Objectives
- Calculate perimeter and area
- Understand angles
- Work with triangles, rectangles, circles
- Calculate volume

### Lesson 6.1: Perimeter & Area

**Perimeter:** Distance around shape

**Rectangle:**
- P = 2l + 2w
- Example: l = 8, w = 5
- P = 2(8) + 2(5) = 16 + 10 = 26

**Square:**
- P = 4s
- Example: s = 6, P = 24

**Area:** Space inside shape

**Rectangle:**
- A = l × w
- Example: l = 8, w = 5, A = 40

**Square:**
- A = s²
- Example: s = 6, A = 36

**Triangle:**
- A = ½ × base × height
- Example: b = 10, h = 6
- A = ½ × 10 × 6 = 30

**Circle:**
- Circumference: C = 2πr or πd
- Area: A = πr²
- π ≈ 3.14

**Example:** Circle with radius 5
- C = 2 × 3.14 × 5 = 31.4
- A = 3.14 × 5² = 3.14 × 25 = 78.5

### Lesson 6.2: Angles & Triangles

**Angle Types:**
- Acute: < 90°
- Right: = 90°
- Obtuse: > 90°
- Straight: = 180°

**Triangle Types:**
- By sides: Equilateral, Isosceles, Scalene
- By angles: Acute, Right, Obtuse

**Angle Sum:** All triangles = 180°

**Pythagorean Theorem:**
For right triangles: a² + b² = c²
(c = hypotenuse, longest side)

**Example:** a = 3, b = 4
- c² = 3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25
- c = √25 = 5

### Lesson 6.3: Volume

**Rectangular Prism:**
V = l × w × h

**Example:** Box 4 × 3 × 2
V = 4 × 3 × 2 = 24 cubic units

**Cylinder:**
V = πr²h

**Example:** r = 3, h = 10
V = 3.14 × 9 × 10 = 282.6

## Module 7: Data Analysis (Week 11)

### Learning Objectives
- Calculate mean, median, mode, range
- Read and create graphs
- Understand probability basics
- Interpret data

### Lesson 7.1: Central Tendency

**Mean (Average):**
Add all numbers, divide by count

**Example:** 8, 12, 15, 5, 10
- Sum: 50
- Count: 5
- Mean: 50 ÷ 5 = 10

**Median (Middle):**
Arrange in order, find middle

**Example:** 3, 7, 8, 12, 15
- Median: 8

**If even count:** Average two middle numbers
- 3, 7, 8, 12 → (7 + 8) ÷ 2 = 7.5

**Mode (Most frequent):**
Number appearing most

**Example:** 2, 3, 3, 5, 7, 3
- Mode: 3

**Range:**
Highest - Lowest

**Example:** 5, 8, 12, 3, 15
- Range: 15 - 3 = 12

### Lesson 7.2: Graphs & Charts

**Types:**
- Bar graphs: Compare categories
- Line graphs: Show trends over time
- Pie charts: Show parts of whole
- Tables: Organize data

**Reading Graphs:**
1. Check title
2. Read axes labels
3. Check scale
4. Interpret data

**Example:** Bar graph showing monthly sales
- January: $5,000
- February: $7,000
- March: $6,000

Questions:
- Best month? February
- Average? ($5K + $7K + $6K) ÷ 3 = $6,000

### Lesson 7.3: Probability

**Probability = Favorable outcomes / Total outcomes**

**Example:** Coin flip
- P(Heads) = 1/2 = 0.5 = 50%

**Example:** Die roll
- P(rolling 3) = 1/6
- P(rolling even) = 3/6 = ½

**Example:** Drawing from deck
- P(red card) = 26/52 = ½
- P(ace) = 4/52 = 1/13

## Success Strategies for Adult Math Learners

1. **Start where you are** - Don't compare to others
2. **Use real-world examples** - Connect to your life
3. **Practice daily** - Even 15 minutes helps
4. **Show your work** - Don't just do mental math
5. **Check answers** - Use calculator to verify
6. **Ask questions** - No shame in not knowing
7. **Use manipulatives** - Physical objects help
8. **Draw pictures** - Visualize problems
9. **Work with others** - Study groups help
10. **Celebrate progress** - Every small win matters

### Overcoming Math Anxiety
- Recognize it's common
- Focus on understanding, not speed
- Use positive self-talk
- Break problems into smaller steps
- Take breaks when frustrated
- Get enough sleep before studying

**Remember:** Math is a skill, not a talent. Anyone can learn with practice!
