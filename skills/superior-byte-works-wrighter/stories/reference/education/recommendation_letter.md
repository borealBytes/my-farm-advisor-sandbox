# Letter of Recommendation Template

> [!IMPORTANT]
> This template supports writing effective recommendation letters. Always obtain student/employee consent before sharing personal information. Follow FERPA guidelines for educational records.

## Pre-Writing Considerations

### Before You Begin

> [!CAUTION]

- Only write recommendations for individuals you know well enough to evaluate honestly. Decline if you cannot provide a strong, specific recommendation.

**Assess Your Ability to Recommend:**

- [ ] Do I know this person well enough to write specifically?
- [ ] Can I provide concrete examples of their abilities?
- [ ] Can I honestly recommend them for this opportunity?
- [ ] Do I have time to write a thoughtful letter?

**If You Must Decline:**

- Respond promptly and politely
- Explain that you cannot provide a strong recommendation
- Suggest others who might be better references
- Do not provide a lukewarm or negative letter

### Gather Information

**From the Candidate:**

- Resume/CV
- Transcript (if applicable)
- Personal statement or cover letter
- Description of the opportunity
- Specific skills or experiences to highlight
- Deadline and submission instructions
- Any forms or questionnaires

**Your Records:**

- Course grades/performance reviews
- Specific interactions or projects
- Dates of association
- Your relationship to the candidate

## Letter Structure

### Format Guidelines

**Length:** 1-2 pages (400-800 words)

**Format:**

- Professional letterhead if available
- Standard business letter format
- 11-12 point professional font
- Single-spaced with space between paragraphs
- 1-inch margins

**Tone:** Professional, enthusiastic, specific, and honest

### Letter Components

#### 1. Header

```
[Your Name]
[Your Title/Position]
[Your Institution/Organization]
[Your Address]
[Your Email]
[Your Phone]

[Date]

[Recipient Name (if known)]
[Recipient Title]
[Organization]
[Address]
```

#### 2. Salutation

**Specific:**

- "Dear Admissions Committee:"
- "Dear Hiring Manager:"
- "Dear [Specific Name]:"

**General:**

- "To Whom It May Concern:"
- "Dear Selection Committee:"

#### 3. Opening Paragraph

> [!TIP]

- State your relationship to the candidate immediately. This establishes your credibility as a recommender.

**Purpose:**

- State your relationship to the candidate
- Indicate how long you've known them
- Describe the context of your relationship
- State your overall recommendation

**Template:**

> "I am writing to recommend [Full Name] for [position/program/opportunity] at [Institution/Organization]. I have known [Name] for [duration] as their [relationship: professor, supervisor, etc.] in [context: course, job, research project]. Based on [his/her/their] exceptional [qualities], I give [Name] my highest recommendation."

**Example:**

> "I am writing to recommend Sarah Chen for the Master's program in Computer Science at Stanford University. I have known Sarah for three years as her research advisor and professor in the Department of Computer Science at State University. Based on her exceptional analytical abilities, dedication to research, and collaborative spirit, I give Sarah my highest recommendation."

#### 4. Body Paragraphs (2-3 paragraphs)

**Paragraph 2: Academic/Professional Competence**

Focus on:

- Intellectual abilities
- Technical skills
- Knowledge in field
- Academic/professional achievements

**Template:**

> "[Name] demonstrates exceptional ability in [specific area]. In my [course/program], [he/she/they] consistently [specific achievement]. For example, [specific instance with details]. [His/Her/Their] work on [project/assignment] showed [specific skills]."

**Paragraph 3: Personal Qualities and Character**

Focus on:

- Work ethic
- Communication skills
- Leadership
- Collaboration
- Integrity
- Growth mindset

**Template:**

> "Beyond [his/her/their] technical abilities, [Name] possesses outstanding [personal qualities]. [Specific example demonstrating quality]. [He/She/They] consistently [behavior that demonstrates character]."

**Paragraph 4: Specific Examples (Optional but Recommended)**

Include:

- Detailed anecdotes
- Quantifiable achievements
- Comparisons to peers
- Growth over time

> [!NOTE]

- Specific examples make letters memorable. "Sarah led a team of 5 to complete a project 2 weeks early" is stronger than "Sarah is a good leader."

#### 5. Closing Paragraph

**Purpose:**

- Reiterate strong recommendation
- Summarize key strengths
- Offer to provide additional information
- Include contact information

**Template:**

> "I am confident that [Name] will excel in [opportunity] and make significant contributions to [program/organization]. [He/She/They] possesses the [key qualities] necessary for success. I recommend [him/her/them] without reservation. Please contact me at [email/phone] if you require additional information."

#### 6. Closing and Signature

```
Sincerely,

[Signature]

[Typed Name]
[Title]
[Institution]
[Email]
[Phone]
```

## Sample Letters

### Academic Recommendation (Graduate School)

---

[Your Letterhead]

[Date]

Graduate Admissions Committee
[Department Name]
[University Name]
[Address]

Dear Admissions Committee:

I am writing to enthusiastically recommend Michael Rodriguez for admission to your Ph.D. program in Physics. I have known Michael for four years, first as his professor in Quantum Mechanics and subsequently as his research advisor. Michael is one of the most talented and dedicated students I have encountered in my fifteen years of teaching.

Michael's intellectual abilities are truly exceptional. In my Advanced Quantum Mechanics course, he not only mastered the complex mathematical formalism but also demonstrated remarkable physical intuition. His final project, which explored quantum entanglement in novel materials, was of publishable quality. He received the highest grade in a class of 45 students, including several graduate students.

What distinguishes Michael is his combination of technical brilliance and collaborative spirit. He served as a peer tutor for two years, helping struggling students understand difficult concepts with patience and clarity. His research contributions to our lab have been substantial; he independently developed a computational model that reduced our simulation time by 40%. This work resulted in a co-authored paper currently under review at Physical Review Letters.

Michael possesses the intellectual curiosity, analytical rigor, and perseverance necessary for doctoral study. I am confident he will make significant contributions to your program and to the field of physics. I give him my highest recommendation without reservation.

Please contact me at professor@university.edu or (555) 123-4567 if you need additional information.

Sincerely,

[Signature]

Dr. Jennifer Walsh
Professor of Physics
State University
Email: professor@university.edu
Phone: (555) 123-4567

---

I am pleased to recommend Alexandra Kim for the position of Marketing Manager at your company. I supervised Alexandra for three years at XYZ Corporation, where she served as Senior Marketing Coordinator and consistently exceeded expectations.

Alexandra is a strategic thinker with exceptional execution skills. She led our product launch campaign last year, which exceeded revenue targets by 35% and won an industry award. Her ability to analyze market data, identify opportunities, and develop creative solutions sets her apart. She managed a $2M budget with complete accountability and transparency.

Beyond her technical marketing expertise, Alexandra is an outstanding team leader. She mentored two junior coordinators who have since been promoted. Her communication skills are excellent; she can present complex data to executives clearly and persuasively. During a critical product recall, she managed communications with professionalism and helped maintain customer trust.

Alexandra would be an asset to your organization. She combines strategic vision with operational excellence and leads with integrity. I recommend her without hesitation.

[Signature]

David Thompson

## Writing Tips

### Do's

- [ ] Be specific with examples
- [ ] Use concrete numbers when possible
- [ ] Address the specific opportunity
- [ ] Highlight relevant strengths
- [ ] Proofread carefully
- [ ] Meet deadlines
- [ ] Follow submission instructions exactly

### Don'ts

- [ ] Don't use generic templates without customization
- [ ] Don't include irrelevant personal information
- [ ] Don't exaggerate or misrepresent
- [ ] Don't use overly casual language
- [ ] Don't include confidential information
- [ ] Don't write if you cannot recommend strongly

### Language Guidelines

**Strong Verbs:**

- Achieved, accomplished, demonstrated, excelled
- Initiated, created, developed, designed
- Led, managed, coordinated, organized
- Analyzed, evaluated, solved, improved

**Avoid:**

- Vague praise without evidence
- Clichés ("hard worker," "team player" without examples)
- Overly effusive language that seems insincere
- Comparisons you cannot support

## FERPA and Privacy Considerations

> [!WARNING]

- Educational records are protected under FERPA. Obtain written consent before disclosing information from education records.

**Required:**

- Student consent for disclosure
- Limit information to what is relevant
- Secure transmission of letters

**Best Practices:**

- Keep copies of consent forms
- Use secure submission methods
- Destroy drafts containing sensitive information
